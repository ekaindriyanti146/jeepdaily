## Hook

The automotive industry witnessed a remarkable shift in 2025, with the Toyota Tacoma achieving its best sales year ever.

The Tacoma's success can be attributed to its impressive capabilities, both on and off the road, as well as its reputation for reliability and durability.

## History/Context

The Toyota Tacoma has a rich history that spans over three decades.

First introduced in 1995, the Tacoma was designed to replace the Toyota Pickup, which had been in production since 1972.

Over the years, the Tacoma has undergone significant transformations, with each generation bringing improvements in design, technology, and performance.

The current third-generation Tacoma, which was introduced in 2016, has been a huge success, thanks to its aggressive styling, advanced safety features, and impressive off-road capabilities.

## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Toyota Tacoma is available in a range of trim levels, each with its unique set of features and capabilities.

The base model SR comes with a 2.7-liter four-cylinder engine, producing 159 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque.

The more advanced TRD Pro model features a 3.5-liter V6 engine, producing 278 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque.

The Tacoma's suspension system is designed to provide a smooth ride on paved roads, while also delivering exceptional off-road capability.

The truck's suspension features a double-wishbone design in the front and a leaf spring setup in the rear, providing a balance of comfort and durability.

The following table highlights the key technical specifications of the Toyota Tacoma:

| TRD Pro | 3.5L V6 | 278 hp | 265 lb-ft | Double-Wishbone (Front), Leaf Spring (Rear) with FOX Shocks |
| Limited | 3.5L V6 | 278 hp | 265 lb-ft | Double-Wishbone (Front), Leaf Spring (Rear) with Adaptive Suspension |

## Real World Performance

The Toyota Tacoma's real-world performance is a testament to its exceptional capabilities.

Whether navigating challenging off-road terrain or cruising down the highway, the Tacoma delivers a smooth and confident ride.

The truck's advanced safety features, including adaptive cruise control and lane departure warning, provide added peace of mind for drivers.

In terms of fuel efficiency, the Tacoma's estimated EPA ratings range from 18 mpg in the city to 22 mpg on the highway, depending on the trim level and engine configuration.

Overall, the Tacoma's performance is a key factor in its enduring popularity and record-breaking sales.

## Comparison/Verdict

The Toyota Tacoma's success in 2025 is a significant achievement, especially considering the intense competition in the mid-size pickup truck market.

GM, Ford, and Jeep have all improved their sales in recent years, but the Tacoma remains a leader in its class.

The Tacoma's unique blend of off-road capability, reliability, and durability has cemented its position as a top choice for truck enthusiasts.

As the automotive industry continues to evolve, it will be interesting to see how the Tacoma adapts to changing market trends and technological advancements.

For now, the Tacoma's record-breaking sales are a testament to its enduring popularity and its status as a legendary pickup truck.
