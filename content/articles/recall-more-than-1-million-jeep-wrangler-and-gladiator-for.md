## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator have a long history of being reliable and durable off-road vehicles.

The Wrangler, in particular, has been a staple of the Jeep brand, with its roots dating back to the 1940s.

The Gladiator, on the other hand, was introduced in 2019 as a pickup truck version of the Wrangler.

Both vehicles have been popular among off-road enthusiasts, with their ability to tackle challenging terrain and withstand harsh conditions.

However, the recent recall has raised concerns about the safety of these vehicles.

The recall affects certain model year 2018-2022 Wrangler and Gladiator vehicles, with the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) citing a risk of fire due to an electrical issue.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The recall is related to an electrical issue with the vehicle's transmission sensor.

According to the NHTSA, the sensor can overheat, causing a fire.

The technical details of the recall are as follows:
| Vehicle | Model Year | Affected Component |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Jeep Wrangler | 2018-2022 | Transmission sensor |
| Jeep Gladiator | 2018-2022 | Transmission sensor |
The engine and suspension of the Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator are not directly affected by the recall.

However, the electrical issue can cause a fire, which can damage the engine and suspension.

The recall will involve the replacement of the transmission sensor, which will be done free of charge by Jeep dealerships.
