The Jeep Wrangler has a rich history dating back to the 1940s, with the Willys MB being the first mass-produced civilian Jeep. Over the years, the Wrangler has undergone numerous transformations, with each generation building upon the previous one. The introduction of the Wrangler Willys 392 marks a new chapter in this legacy, combining the classic design with modern technology and a potent engine. The Willys 392 is a tribute to the original Willys MB, with its name paying homage to the legendary 4x4 vehicle.

The Wrangler Willys 392 is not just a special edition, but a celebration of Jeep's heritage and commitment to off-road excellence. The Wrangler Willys 392 is powered by a 6.4-liter Hemi V8 engine, producing 470 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque. This potent powerplant is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, featuring a heavy-duty torque converter and paddle-shift technology. The engine is also equipped with a functional hood scoop, which provides additional cooling and enhances the vehicle's aggressive stance.

The suspension system has been upgraded to handle the increased power and torque, featuring a heavy-duty Dana 44 front axle and a Dana 44HD rear axle. The Willys 392 also boasts a unique suspension tuning, with hydraulic shock absorbers and a rear stabilizer bar. This setup provides a smooth ride on paved roads, while maintaining the Wrangler's legendary off-road capabilities.
