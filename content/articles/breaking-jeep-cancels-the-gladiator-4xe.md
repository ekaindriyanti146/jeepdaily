## Hook

The automotive world was shocked when Jeep announced the cancellation of the Gladiator 4xe, a plug-in hybrid electric vehicle (PHEV) that was supposed to revolutionize the truck market.

With its impressive capabilities and eco-friendly features, the Gladiator 4xe had generated a lot of buzz among truck enthusiasts and environmentalists alike.

But what led to its demise, and what does this mean for the future of electric trucks? In this article, we'll delve into the history and evolution of the Jeep Gladiator 4xe, exploring its technical details, real-world performance, and how it compares to other electric trucks on the market.

## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Gladiator 4xe was first unveiled in 2020, as part of Jeep's plan to electrify its entire lineup.

The company had announced that it would offer a hybrid or electric version of every model by 2025, and the Gladiator 4xe was one of the first steps towards achieving this goal.

The truck was designed to combine the best of both worlds, offering the off-road capability and toughness of a traditional Jeep, along with the efficiency and eco-friendliness of an electric vehicle.

With a estimated 25 miles of electric-only range and a total range of over 400 miles, the Gladiator 4xe seemed like the perfect solution for those who wanted to go green without sacrificing performance.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Jeep Gladiator 4xe was powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine, paired with an electric motor and a 17-kilowatt-hour battery pack.

The truck's hybrid system produced a total of 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque, making it capable of towing up to 7,650 pounds.

The Gladiator 4xe also featured a unique suspension system, with a combination of coil springs and Fox shocks that provided a smooth ride on the road and excellent articulation off-road.

The truck's electric motor was powered by a lithium-ion battery pack, which could be charged via a 240-volt Level 2 charger or a DC Fast Charger.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

So how did the Jeep Gladiator 4xe perform in the real world? According to reviews and test drives, the truck was a mixed bag.

On the one hand, its electric motor provided instant torque and excellent acceleration, making it feel quick and responsive on the road.

The truck's suspension system also proved to be highly capable, providing a smooth ride on paved roads and excellent articulation on off-road trails.

However, the Gladiator 4xe's battery pack was relatively small, which limited its electric-only range and required frequent charging.

Additionally, the truck's hybrid system was complex and expensive to produce, which may have contributed to its cancellation.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

So how does the Jeep Gladiator 4xe compare to other electric trucks on the market? The Rivian R1T, for example, offers a similar combination of off-road capability and electric power, but with a more extensive range and better overall performance.

The Ford F-150 Lightning, on the other hand, offers a more traditional truck experience, with a larger battery pack and a more extensive range.

Ultimately, the cancellation of the Gladiator 4xe is a setback for Jeep and the electric truck market as a whole.

However, it's also an opportunity for the company to regroup and reassess its electrification strategy, with the goal of producing better and more competitive electric vehicles in the future.
