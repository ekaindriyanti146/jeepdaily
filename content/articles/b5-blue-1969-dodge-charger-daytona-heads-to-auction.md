# Introduction to the 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona

The 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona is one of the most iconic American muscle cars in history, known for its powerful engine and distinctive wing design. However, one particular example, known as the 'Felon Daytona,' has a story that is even more unusual than most. This B5 Blue Metallic 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona has been restored to its former glory and is now heading to auction, giving collectors a rare chance to own a piece of automotive history.

## History and Context of the 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona

The 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona was produced in limited numbers, with only 503 units made. It was designed to compete in NASCAR, with its winged design providing increased downforce and stability at high speeds. The 'Felon Daytona' got its name from its unusual history, which involved a title-fraud investigation that left it sitting in a Kansas City salvage yard for nearly a decade. Despite its troubled past, the car has been fully restored and is now ready for a new owner.

## Real World Performance of the 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona

The 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona is a highly capable performance car, with its powerful engine and rear-wheel drive layout making it well-suited to spirited driving.
