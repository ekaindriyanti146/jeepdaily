## History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler has a rich history dating back to the 1940s, with its iconic design and off-road prowess making it a staple of American automotive culture. The Gladiator, on the other hand, was first introduced in 1962 as a pickup truck variant of the Wrangler. Over the years, both models have undergone significant transformations, but their core DNA has remained unchanged. The Rewind Editions are a tribute to the '90s, with design cues and features that pay homage to the decade of grunge music and high-waisted jeans.

The '90s were a pivotal time for the Jeep brand, with the introduction of the Wrangler TJ in 1996 marking a significant shift towards a more modern and refined design. The Gladiator, however, was discontinued in the '90s, only to be revived in 2019 as a 2020 model. The Rewind Editions are a celebration of this heritage, with unique exterior and interior design elements that evoke the spirit of the '90s.

## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The 2026 Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator Rewind Editions are built on the same platform as their standard counterparts, with a few key differences. The Rewind Editions feature a unique exterior design package, including a distinctive grille, bumpers, and wheels. Inside, the vehicles boast retro-inspired interior trim, including a vintage-style instrument cluster and premium cloth seats.

In terms of performance, the Rewind Editions are available with a range of engine options, including a 3.6L V6 and a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four. The vehicles also feature a robust suspension system, with a five-link coil suspension setup in the rear and a Dana 44 axle.
