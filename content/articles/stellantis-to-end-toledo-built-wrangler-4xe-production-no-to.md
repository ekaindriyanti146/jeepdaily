## Hook

The automotive world has been abuzz with the recent announcement from Stellantis, the parent company of Jeep, that it will be ending the production of the Wrangler 4xe in Toledo, Ohio. This news has sent shockwaves through the off-road community, with many enthusiasts wondering what this means for the future of the iconic Wrangler. As a senior automotive journalist, I'm here to provide a comprehensive review of the Wrangler 4xe, its history, technical details, and real-world performance.

## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler has a rich history dating back to 1986, with the first generation debuting as a replacement for the CJ series.

Over the years, the Wrangler has undergone several transformations, with each new generation bringing significant improvements in design, performance, and technology.

The Wrangler 4xe, in particular, represents a significant milestone in the model's history, as it marks the first time a Wrangler has been offered with a plug-in hybrid powertrain.

This innovative approach has made the Wrangler 4xe an attractive option for those seeking a capable off-road vehicle with reduced emissions.

The Wrangler 4xe's production in Toledo, Ohio, has been a significant contributor to the local economy, with the plant employing thousands of workers. The announcement of the production's end has raised concerns about the potential impact on local jobs. However, Stellantis has reassured the community that the decision will not affect employment levels, as the plant will continue to produce other Jeep models.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Wrangler 4xe boasts an impressive array of technical features, including a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four engine, paired with an electric motor and a 17-kWh lithium-ion battery pack. This powertrain combination produces an impressive 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque, making it one of the most capable Wranglers to date. The 4xe also features a sophisticated all-wheel-drive system, complete with a two-speed transfer case and a locking rear differential.

The Wrangler 4xe's suspension is equally impressive, with a robust five-link coil suspension setup in the front and a trailing arm setup in the rear. This combination provides exceptional articulation and stability, making the Wrangler 4xe a formidable off-road competitor. ## Section 3: Real World Performance

In real-world testing, the Wrangler 4xe has proven to be an exceptional off-road performer, with its advanced all-wheel-drive system and impressive ground clearance allowing it to tackle even the most challenging terrain. The Wrangler 4xe's electric motor provides instant torque, making it ideal for rock crawling and other low-speed off-road applications.

On the road, the Wrangler 4xe is equally impressive, with its smooth and quiet powertrain providing a comfortable driving experience. The Wrangler 4xe's regenerative braking system also helps to recharge the battery pack, allowing for extended electric-only driving ranges.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

The Wrangler 4xe is a unique offering in the off-road market, with its plug-in hybrid powertrain and advanced all-wheel-drive system making it an attractive option for those seeking a capable and environmentally friendly vehicle. While the announcement of the production's end in Toledo, Ohio, may come as a disappointment to some, it's clear that the Wrangler 4xe has left an indelible mark on the off-road community.

In comparison to other off-road vehicles, the Wrangler 4xe stands out for its exceptional capability, comfort, and innovative powertrain. While it may not be the most affordable option, the Wrangler 4xe's unique blend of performance, technology, and sustainability make it a compelling choice for those seeking a premium off-road experience.

No, according to Stellantis, the decision will not impact employment levels, as the plant will continue to produce other Jeep models.
3. What is the Wrangler 4xe's ground clearance?
The Wrangler 4xe has a ground clearance of 10.8 inches.
