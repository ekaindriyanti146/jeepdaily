## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler has a rich history that spans over three decades.

First introduced in 1986, the Wrangler was designed to be a direct descendant of the World War II-era Willys MB.

Over the years, the Wrangler has undergone significant transformations, with each new generation building upon the successes of the previous one.

The 2026 model is no exception, boasting improved performance, enhanced off-road capabilities, and a range of innovative features.

The Jeep Wrangler has become an iconic symbol of adventure and freedom, with a loyal following of enthusiasts who appreciate its unique blend of style, capability, and ruggedness.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The 2026 Jeep Wrangler is available with a range of engine options, including a 3.6-liter V6 and a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four.

The V6 engine produces 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, while the turbocharged engine produces 270 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque.

Both engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission, although a six-speed manual is available on certain trim levels.

The Wrangler's suspension has been redesigned to provide improved on-road comfort and off-road capability, with a new five-link rear suspension and revised front suspension geometry.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

The 2026 Jeep Wrangler is a highly capable vehicle that excels in a variety of environments.

On the road, it provides a comfortable and composed ride, with precise steering and responsive handling.

Off the road, the Wrangler is in its element, with a range of four-wheel-drive systems and advanced traction control technologies that enable it to tackle even the most challenging terrain.

The Wrangler's approach and departure angles have been improved, allowing it to navigate steep inclines and declines with ease.

The vehicle's ground clearance has also been increased, providing additional protection from rocks and other obstacles.
