## History/Context

The Jeep Gladiator has a long and storied history, dating back to the original Willys Jeep of World War II. Over the years, the Gladiator has evolved to become one of the most capable and iconic off-road vehicles on the market. The introduction of the 4xe plug-in hybrid powertrain marks a major milestone in this evolution, as Jeep seeks to balance its commitment to off-road performance with the need for greater environmental sustainability. With the 4xe, Jeep is targeting a new generation of off-road enthusiasts who are increasingly concerned about the environmental impact of their vehicles.

## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

So what exactly does the Gladiator 4xe plug-in hybrid bring to the table? At its heart is a powerful and efficient powertrain, combining a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four engine with two electric motors.

This setup produces a total system output of 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque, making the 4xe one of the most capable off-road vehicles in its class.

The 4xe also features a advanced suspension system, with a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil suspension with a stabilizer bar in the rear.

This setup provides a smooth and comfortable ride on the road, while also allowing for exceptional articulation and control off-road.
