The Jeep Gladiator is known for its impressive technical capabilities, including a powerful engine and a robust suspension system.

The current model features a 3.6-liter V6 engine, producing 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque.

The Gladiator also boasts a capable suspension system, with a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil suspension with a stabilizer bar in the rear.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

The Jeep Gladiator is renowned for its exceptional off-road performance, with a wading depth of up to 30 inches and a ground clearance of 11.1 inches.

The vehicle's approach and departure angles are also impressive, with 43.6 degrees and 26 degrees, respectively.

While the electrified version was expected to offer improved performance and efficiency, the cancellation of the project means that enthusiasts will have to wait for other electric off-road vehicles to hit the market.

The real-world implications of this cancellation are significant, with many wondering about the future of electric technology in the off-road segment.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

The cancellation of the electrified Jeep Gladiator has significant implications for the off-road industry. With other manufacturers, such as Rivian and Tesla, investing heavily in electric off-road vehicles, Stellantis's decision may be seen as a missed opportunity.
