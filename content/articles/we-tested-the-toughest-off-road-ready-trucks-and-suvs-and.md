### The CJ and Wrangler Era
In the 1950s, Jeep introduced the CJ (Civilian Jeep) series, which was designed for civilian use. The CJ series was produced for over 40 years and became a iconic symbol of off-road adventure. In the 1980s, Jeep introduced the Wrangler, which was designed to be a more modern and comfortable version of the CJ. The Wrangler quickly became a best-seller and has remained one of the most popular off-road vehicles on the market.

#### Technical Breakdown
The Wrangler's success can be attributed to its rugged design and impressive off-road capabilities. The Wrangler features a robust 4x4 system, which includes a two-speed transfer case and a locking rear axle. The vehicle also features a high ground clearance of 10.8 inches, which allows it to navigate rocky and uneven terrain with ease.

## Engine Updates and Performance
Over the years, Jeep has made significant updates to the Wrangler's engine, including the introduction of a 3.6L V6 engine and a 2.0L turbocharged engine. The 3.6L V6 engine produces 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.0L turbocharged engine produces 270 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque.
