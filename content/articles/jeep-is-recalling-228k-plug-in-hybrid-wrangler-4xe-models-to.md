## Hook

The Jeep Wrangler 4xe, a plug-in hybrid version of the iconic off-road vehicle, has been making waves in the automotive world with its impressive performance and eco-friendly features. However, a recent recall notice has sent shockwaves through the off-road community, with over 228,000 units affected due to a potential fire risk. As a senior automotive journalist, I will delve into the history of the Wrangler 4xe, its technical details, and the implications of this recall on its off-road performance.

## History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler 4xe was first introduced in 2020 as a 2021 model, marking a significant milestone in the brand's electrification efforts.

The Wrangler 4xe combines a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine with an electric motor, producing a total system output of 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque.

This powertrain is paired with a eight-speed automatic transmission and a four-wheel-drive system, making it an attractive option for off-road enthusiasts.

With its impressive performance capabilities and eco-friendly features, the Wrangler 4xe has been well-received by critics and consumers alike.

## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Wrangler 4xe's powertrain is a key aspect of its off-road performance.

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces 270 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, while the electric motor contributes an additional 105 horsepower and 134 lb-ft of torque.

The battery pack, which consists of 17 kWh lithium-ion cells, provides an estimated 25 miles of electric-only range.

The Wrangler 4xe also features a robust suspension system, with a five-link coil suspension setup in the front and a five-link coil suspension setup with a stabilizer bar in the rear.

The vehicle's ground clearance and approach/departure angles are also impressive, with 10.8 inches of clearance and 35.6/29.2 degrees of approach/departure angle, respectively.

## Real World Performance

In real-world testing, the Wrangler 4xe has proven to be a capable off-road performer, with its instant torque and generous ground clearance allowing it to tackle challenging terrain with ease. The vehicle's electric motor also provides a significant boost in low-speed maneuverability, making it ideal for rock crawling and other low-speed off-road activities. However, the recall notice has raised concerns about the vehicle's safety, with the potential fire risk posing a significant threat to drivers and passengers.

## Comparison/Verdict

The Jeep Wrangler 4xe is a unique offering in the off-road market, with its plug-in hybrid powertrain and impressive performance capabilities.

However, the recall notice has cast a shadow over the vehicle's reputation, with many off-road enthusiasts left wondering about its safety and reliability.

In comparison to other off-road vehicles on the market, the Wrangler 4xe is still a top contender, with its impressive ground clearance, approach/departure angles, and low-speed maneuverability.

However, the recall notice has highlighted the need for Jeep to address the potential fire risk and ensure the vehicle's safety and reliability.
