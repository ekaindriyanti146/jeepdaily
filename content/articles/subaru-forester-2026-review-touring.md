## Hook

The Subaru Forester has long been a staple in the compact SUV market, known for its all-wheel-drive capability and ruggedness. For 2026, Subaru has refined its formula, introducing the Touring trim that promises to elevate the Forester's off-road performance. But does it live up to the hype? In this review, we'll delve into the Forester's history, its technical details, and put it to the test in real-world off-road scenarios.

## Section 1: History/Context

The Subaru Forester was first introduced in 1997, as a compact SUV that could handle both on-road comfort and off-road adventures. Over the years, it has undergone several transformations, with each generation building upon the last. The current fifth generation, introduced in 2019, marked a significant leap forward in terms of design, safety features, and performance. The 2026 model year sees the introduction of the Touring trim, which Subaru aims to position as the pinnacle of Forester capability and luxury.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

At the heart of the 2026 Subaru Forester Touring is a 2.5-liter naturally aspirated boxer engine, producing 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT) and Subaru's legendary Symmetrical All-Wheel Drive system. The suspension has been tuned for both comfort and capability, with MacPherson struts up front and a double-wishbone setup at the rear. The Touring trim comes equipped with 18-inch alloy wheels wrapped in 225/60R18 all-season tires, providing a good balance between on-road manners and off-road traction.

| **Specification** | **Detail** |
| --- | --- |

| Drive Type | Symmetrical AWD |
| Suspension (Front/Rear) | MacPherson Struts / Double Wishbone |
| Wheels | 18-inch Alloy |
| Tires | 225/60R18 All-Season |

## Section 3: Real World Performance

In real-world testing, the Subaru Forester Touring proved itself to be more than capable off the beaten path. Its all-wheel-drive system and generous ground clearance of 8.7 inches allowed it to tackle challenging terrain with ease, including steep inclines, rocky trails, and muddy bogs. The CVT, while not the most engaging transmission for spirited driving, provided seamless power delivery in off-road scenarios, allowing the Forester to crawl over obstacles with confidence.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

Compared to its competitors in the compact SUV segment, the Subaru Forester Touring stands out for its genuine off-road capability and standard all-wheel drive. While others may offer AWD as an option or tout their vehicles as 'rugged' without the hardware to back it up, the Forester delivers. It's not the most powerful or the most luxurious, but for those seeking a vehicle that can handle both daily driving duties and weekend adventures with equal aplomb, the Forester Touring is a compelling choice.
