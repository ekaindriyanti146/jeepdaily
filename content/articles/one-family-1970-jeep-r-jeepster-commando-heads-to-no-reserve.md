## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeepster Commando was first introduced in 1966, as a more civilian-friendly version of the classic Jeep.

It was designed to be a versatile vehicle, capable of handling both on-road and off-road duties with ease.

The Commando was available in a range of body styles, including a pickup truck, a convertible, and a wagon.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The 1970 Jeepster Commando is powered by a 3.8L inline-six engine, producing 160 horsepower and 235 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a three-speed manual transmission, which sends power to all four wheels via a two-speed transfer case. The Commando features a live front axle and a live rear axle, with leaf springs and shock absorbers providing a smooth ride on and off the road. ## Section 3: Real World Performance

On the road, the 1970 Jeepster Commando is a capable and comfortable vehicle, with a smooth ride and responsive handling. Off the road, the Commando truly comes alive, with its four-wheel drive system and high ground clearance allowing it to tackle even the toughest terrain. The vehicle's live axles and leaf springs provide a smooth ride, even on rough terrain, while the 3.8L engine provides plenty of power for climbing steep inclines. Whether you're cruising down the highway or crawling over rocks, the Jeepster Commando is a joy to drive.
