## Hook

The automotive world is abuzz with the recent recall of over 1 million Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator vehicles due to a power steering wiring fire risk. This recall, issued by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA), affects certain model year 2018-2022 Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator vehicles. As a senior automotive journalist, I will delve into the details of this recall, exploring its causes, the necessary repairs, and what this means for owners of these vehicles.

## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator are two of the most iconic vehicles in the Jeep lineup, known for their ruggedness, off-road capability, and distinctive styling.

However, like all vehicles, they are not immune to defects and recalls.

In recent years, Jeep has issued several recalls for various issues, including problems with the transmission, engine, and electrical systems.

This latest recall, however, is particularly concerning due to the risk of fire.

The recall affects a total of 1,025,280 vehicles in the United States, with an additional 160,000 vehicles affected in other countries.

The recall is the result of an investigation by the NHTSA, which found that the power steering wiring harness may be prone to overheating, potentially causing a fire.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The power steering wiring harness is a critical component of the vehicle's electrical system, responsible for providing power to the power steering pump.

In the affected vehicles, the wiring harness may be damaged due to excessive heat, which can cause the wires to melt and potentially start a fire.

The problem is attributed to a design flaw in the wiring harness, which can cause the wires to rub against other components, generating heat and potentially causing a fire.

To repair the issue, dealers will need to inspect the power steering wiring harness and replace it if necessary.

Additionally, dealers will need to update the vehicle's software to prevent the power steering pump from operating when the vehicle is turned off.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

The recall has significant implications for owners of the affected vehicles.

While the risk of fire is relatively low, it is still a serious issue that requires immediate attention.

Owners of the affected vehicles are advised to contact their local dealers to schedule a repair as soon as possible.

In the meantime, owners can take steps to minimize the risk of fire, such as avoiding extreme temperatures and ensuring that the vehicle is properly maintained.

The recall has also raised concerns about the overall reliability and safety of the Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator.

While both vehicles have a reputation for being rugged and reliable, this recall highlights the importance of regular maintenance and inspections to prevent potential issues.
