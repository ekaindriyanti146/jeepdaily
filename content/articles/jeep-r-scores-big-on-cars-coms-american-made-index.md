## Hook

The American automotive industry has long been a symbol of innovation and manufacturing prowess. As the United States celebrates its 250th anniversary, it's fitting that Cars.com releases its 21st annual American-Made Index, highlighting the best of American-made vehicles. This year, Jeep stands out as a top performer, with both the Jeep Gladiator and Jeep Grand Cherokee family making a strong showing. But what does it mean to be 'American-made,' and how do these Jeep models stack up against the competition?

## History/Context

The concept of an 'American-made' vehicle is complex, involving factors such as the location of the manufacturing plant, the source of the parts, and the overall impact on the American economy.

Cars.com's American-Made Index takes these factors into account, providing a comprehensive ranking of vehicles based on their 'American-ness.' With the rise of globalization, the notion of a purely American-made vehicle has become increasingly rare.

However, Jeep's commitment to domestic manufacturing has paid off, with the Gladiator and Grand Cherokee being produced in the United States.

The American-Made Index is not just a celebration of American manufacturing; it's also a reflection of the industry's shift towards more sustainable and responsible practices.

## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Jeep Gladiator and Grand Cherokee are both renowned for their exceptional off-road capabilities, thanks in part to their advanced engine and suspension systems. The Gladiator boasts a 3.6L V6 engine, producing 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, while the Grand Cherokee offers a range of engine options, including a 5.7L V8 and a 3.6L V6. The suspension systems on both vehicles feature a five-link coil suspension, providing a smooth ride and superior articulation. The following table highlights the key technical specifications of the Jeep Gladiator and Grand Cherokee:

| Model | Engine | Horsepower | Torque | Suspension |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Jeep Gladiator | 3.6L V6 | 285 | 260 lb-ft | Five-link coil suspension |
| Jeep Grand Cherokee | 3.6L V6 | 295 | 260 lb-ft | Five-link coil suspension |
| Jeep Grand Cherokee | 5.7L V8 | 360 | 390 lb-ft | Five-link coil suspension |

The combination of powerful engines and advanced suspension systems makes the Jeep Gladiator and Grand Cherokee well-suited for both on-road and off-road driving.

## Real World Performance

In real-world testing, the Jeep Gladiator and Grand Cherokee have consistently impressed with their exceptional performance and capability.

The Gladiator's towing capacity of up to 7,650 pounds makes it an ideal choice for those who need to haul heavy loads, while the Grand Cherokee's wading depth of up to 24 inches makes it a top pick for off-road enthusiasts.

Both vehicles have also received high praise for their comfortable and feature-rich interiors, making them well-suited for daily driving.

Whether navigating the challenges of the trail or cruising down the highway, the Jeep Gladiator and Grand Cherokee deliver exceptional performance and capability.

## Comparison/Verdict

While Tesla may have taken the top two spots on Cars.com's American-Made Index, the Jeep Gladiator and Grand Cherokee are undeniable winners.

With their commitment to domestic manufacturing and exceptional performance capabilities, these vehicles embody the spirit of American automotive manufacturing.

As the industry continues to evolve, it's clear that Jeep will remain a leader in the field, producing high-quality, American-made vehicles that meet the needs of drivers everywhere.

In conclusion, the Jeep Gladiator and Grand Cherokee are a testament to the power of American manufacturing, and their strong showing on the American-Made Index is a celebration of all that is great about the American automotive industry.
