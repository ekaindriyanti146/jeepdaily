## Section 2: Technical Details, Engine, and Suspension

The Jeep Wrangler 4xe features a complex powertrain system, consisting of a gasoline engine, electric motor, and battery pack. The electric motor adds an additional 105 horsepower and 134 lb-ft of torque, bringing the total system output to 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque. The vehicle's suspension system is designed to provide a smooth ride on paved roads, while also being capable of handling demanding off-road terrain.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

In real-world testing, the Jeep Wrangler 4xe has proven to be a highly capable off-road performer. The vehicle's four-wheel drive system and impressive ground clearance make it well-suited for navigating challenging terrain. However, the recent software update has had a significant impact on the vehicle's performance. Many owners have reported experiencing powertrain glitches, including sudden losses of power and erratic transmission behavior.

These issues have been particularly problematic for owners who use their vehicles for off-road adventures, as the glitches can occur at critical moments, such as when navigating steep inclines or rocky terrain. In some cases, the glitches have even caused the vehicle to become stuck or lose traction, posing a significant safety risk to the occupants.
