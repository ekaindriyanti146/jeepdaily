## Hook

The Jeep Wrangler, an iconic off-road vehicle, has been a staple in the automotive world for decades. However, its safety record has been a subject of concern for many potential buyers. The Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS) has been critical of the Wrangler's performance in crash tests, citing its poor roof strength and lack of standard safety features. But, according to recent reports, Jeep has finally addressed the biggest issue with the Wrangler, and the IIHS has taken notice.

## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler has a long history dating back to the 1940s, when it was first introduced as a military vehicle.

Over the years, the Wrangler has undergone many transformations, but its core philosophy has remained the same - to provide a rugged, off-road capable vehicle that can handle the toughest terrain.

However, as the automotive industry has evolved, safety has become a major concern, and the Wrangler has struggled to keep up.

The IIHS has been testing vehicles since 1995, and the Wrangler has consistently received poor ratings.

In 2019, the Wrangler received a "poor" rating in the IIHS's small overlap front test, which simulates a crash into a tree or pole.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

So, what has changed? According to Jeep, the 2024 Wrangler will feature a host of updates, including a new roof design that provides improved strength and protection in the event of a rollover.

The Wrangler will also come standard with a range of safety features, including blind-spot monitoring, rear cross-traffic alert, and forward collision warning.

The Wrangler's engine options will remain the same, with a choice of a 3.6-liter V6 or a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder.

The suspension system has also been updated, with a new five-link coil suspension setup that provides improved on-road handling and off-road capability.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

So, how do these updates translate to real-world performance? According to the IIHS, the 2024 Wrangler has received a "good" rating in the small overlap front test, which is a significant improvement over the previous model.

The Wrangler has also received a "good" rating in the moderate overlap front test, as well as the side and roof strength tests.

In terms of on-road handling, the Wrangler's new suspension setup provides a smoother ride and improved handling, making it more suitable for daily driving.

The Wrangler's off-road capability remains unchanged, with a range of four-wheel drive systems and a ground clearance of up to 10.8 inches.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

So, how does the 2024 Jeep Wrangler compare to its competitors? The Wrangler's main competitors are the Toyota 4Runner and the Land Rover Defender, both of which offer similar off-road capability and safety features.

However, the Wrangler's updated roof design and standard safety features give it an edge in terms of safety.

In terms of price, the Wrangler starts at around $30,000, which is comparable to the 4Runner and Defender.

Overall, the 2024 Jeep Wrangler is a significant improvement over the previous model, and its updated safety features make it a more appealing option for buyers who prioritize safety.
