The Jeep Gladiator has a long and storied history, dating back to the 1960s.

The original Gladiator was introduced in 1962 as a full-size pickup truck, designed to compete with the likes of Ford and Chevrolet.

Over the years, the Gladiator underwent several transformations, with the most recent iteration being introduced in 2020.

The 2020 Jeep Gladiator is a mid-size pickup truck, built on the same platform as the Jeep Wrangler.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The 2020 Jeep Gladiator is available with two engine options: a 3.6-liter V6 and a 3.0-liter EcoDiesel V6.

The 3.6-liter V6 produces 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.0-liter EcoDiesel V6 produces 260 horsepower and 442 lb-ft of torque.

The Gladiator features a robust suspension system, with a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link leaf spring suspension in the rear.

This suspension setup provides excellent articulation and control, making the Gladiator capable of tackling even the most challenging off-road terrain.

The following table provides a detailed comparison of the Gladiator's engine and suspension options:

**What is the towing capacity of the 2020 Jeep Gladiator?**

**Is the 2020 Jeep Gladiator available with four-wheel drive?**
Yes, the 2020 Jeep Gladiator is available with a four-wheel-drive system, which provides excellent traction and control in a wide range of driving conditions.
3.

**What is the fuel economy of the 2020 Jeep Gladiator?**
The fuel economy of the 2020 Jeep Gladiator varies depending on the engine and drivetrain.
