The Gladiator is based on the Jeep Wrangler platform but features a longer wheelbase and a pickup truck bed. Since its launch, the Gladiator has received widespread acclaim for its off-road prowess, towing capacity, and impressive payload. The Gladiator has also been recognized for its advanced safety features, including forward collision warning, adaptive cruise control, and blind-spot monitoring.

## Section 2: Technical Details, Engine, and Suspension

The Jeep Gladiator is available with two engine options: a 3.6-liter V6 and a 3.0-liter V6 diesel. The 3.6-liter V6 engine produces 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.0-liter V6 diesel engine produces 260 horsepower and 442 lb-ft of torque. The Gladiator also features a robust suspension system, including a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil suspension with a stabilizer bar in the rear. The Gladiator's suspension is designed to provide a smooth ride on paved roads while also delivering exceptional off-road capability.
