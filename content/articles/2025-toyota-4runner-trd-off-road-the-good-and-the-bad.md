The Toyota 4Runner has a rich history dating back to 1984, when it was first introduced as a compact SUV. Over the years, the 4Runner has undergone significant transformations, with each generation bringing new features, technologies, and capabilities. The TRD Off-Road trim, in particular, has been a staple of the 4Runner lineup, offering a unique blend of on-road comfort and off-road prowess. The 2025 model year marks a significant milestone for the 4Runner, as it receives a major update with new exterior styling, updated interior features, and enhanced off-road capabilities.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The 2025 Toyota 4Runner TRD Off-Road is powered by a 4.0-liter V6 engine, producing 270 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque.

The engine is paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission and a two-speed transfer case, allowing for seamless transitions between two-wheel drive and four-wheel drive.

The suspension system features a double-wishbone design in the front and a four-link setup in the rear, providing a smooth ride on paved roads and ample articulation on uneven terrain.

The TRD Off-Road trim also comes with a range of exclusive features, including a locking rear differential, crawl control, and multi-terrain select.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

In real-world off-road scenarios, the 2025 Toyota 4Runner TRD Off-Road proves to be a capable and confident performer. The vehicle's four-wheel-drive system and locking rear differential work in tandem to provide exceptional traction and control, even on steep inclines and rocky terrain. The crawl control feature is particularly useful, allowing the driver to focus on navigating obstacles while the vehicle manages speed and traction. On paved roads, the 4Runner exhibits a smooth and quiet ride, with minimal body roll and responsive handling.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

The 2025 Toyota 4Runner TRD Off-Road is a formidable competitor in the mid-size SUV segment, offering a unique blend of on-road comfort and off-road capability. While it may not be as agile as some of its rivals, the 4Runner's rugged design and advanced four-wheel-drive system make it an attractive option for those who prioritize off-road performance. In comparison to other vehicles in its class, the 4Runner TRD Off-Road stands out for its exceptional ground clearance, approach and departure angles, and range of exclusive features.
