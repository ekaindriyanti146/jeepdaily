The first Jeep truck model, the Willys MB, was introduced in 1941 as a military vehicle.

After the war, the Jeep brand was acquired by Kaiser Motors, which began producing civilian versions of the Willys MB.

The first civilian Jeep truck model, the Jeep CJ-2, was introduced in 1945 and was designed for farming and other agricultural applications.
