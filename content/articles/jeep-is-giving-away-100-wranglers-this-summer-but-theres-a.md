## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler has a rich history dating back to World War II. The first Jeep, the Willys MB, was designed to meet the Army's need for a lightweight, versatile vehicle that could navigate challenging terrain. Over the years, the Jeep has evolved, but its core values have remained the same: ruggedness, reliability, and a passion for adventure. The Jeep Wrangler, in particular, has become an iconic symbol of off-road capability and freedom. With its removable doors and top, it's the perfect vehicle for those who want to connect with nature and push their limits.

The Wrangler comes with a choice of two engines: a 3.6-liter V6 and a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four.

Both engines provide ample power and torque, but the V6 is the more popular choice among off-road enthusiasts.

The Wrangler's suspension is equally impressive, with a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link leaf spring suspension in the rear.

This setup provides a smooth ride on the road and unparalleled stability off-road.

| Drive Type | Four-wheel drive |
