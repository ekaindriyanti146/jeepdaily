## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler has a long and storied history that dates back to World War II.

The first Jeep, the Willys MB, was produced in 1941 and was designed to be a versatile and reliable vehicle for military use.

Over the years, the Jeep has evolved and been refined, with the Wrangler model being introduced in 1986.

Since then, the Wrangler has become a symbol of off-road capability and adventure, with its distinctive design and impressive performance.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The 2025 Jeep Wrangler 2-Door Sport S is powered by a 3.6L V6 engine that produces 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque.

This engine is paired with a 6-speed manual transmission, although an 8-speed automatic is also available.

The Wrangler features a robust suspension system, with a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link leaf spring suspension in the rear.

This suspension system provides excellent articulation and stability, making it ideal for off-road driving.

The Wrangler also features a range of advanced technology features, including a touchscreen infotainment system, Bluetooth connectivity, and a range of safety features such as forward collision warning and blind spot monitoring.
