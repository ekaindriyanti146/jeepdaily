The automotive world has been left stunned by Stellantis' unexpected decision to discontinue the Gladiator 4Xe, a plug-in hybrid electric vehicle (PHEV) that was gaining popularity. ## History and Context of the Gladiator 4Xe

The Gladiator 4Xe was first introduced in 2020 as a variant of the Jeep Gladiator, a mid-size pickup truck. The 4Xe was designed to provide a more environmentally friendly option for off-road enthusiasts, with a plug-in hybrid powertrain that combined a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine with an electric motor. The vehicle was well-received by critics and consumers alike, praised for its impressive off-road capabilities and reduced emissions. However, despite its popularity, Stellantis has decided to discontinue the Gladiator 4Xe, citing reasons that are still unclear.

## Technical Details and Engine of the Gladiator 4Xe

The Gladiator 4Xe boasted an impressive technical specification, with a combined output of 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque. The vehicle featured a 17-kilowatt-hour battery pack, which provided an estimated 25 miles of electric-only range. ## Real World Performance of the Gladiator 4Xe

In real-world testing, the Gladiator 4Xe proved to be a capable and versatile vehicle, both on and off the road. The vehicle's electric motor provided instant torque, making it well-suited for low-speed off-road maneuvers. The 4Xe's suspension system also performed admirably, providing a smooth ride on paved roads and capable handling on rough terrain. However, the vehicle's weight and size made it less agile than some of its competitors, and its fuel economy was not significantly improved over traditional gasoline-powered vehicles.

## Comparison and Verdict

The Gladiator 4Xe's discontinuation has left a gap in the market for a capable off-road PHEV vehicle. Competitors such as the Ford F-150 Hybrid and the Toyota RAV4 Prime offer similar capabilities, but with different strengths and weaknesses. The F-150 Hybrid, for example, offers more towing capacity and a more extensive range of trim levels, but at a higher price point. The RAV4 Prime, on the other hand, offers a more compact and fuel-efficient package, but with less off-road capability.

In conclusion, the Gladiator 4Xe's discontinuation is a surprising move, given its popularity and the growing demand for PHEV vehicles. While the vehicle had its flaws, it filled a unique niche in the market, and its absence will be felt by off-road enthusiasts.
