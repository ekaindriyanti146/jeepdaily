# The Best of 'On the Trail' 2021: Conquering the U.S.'s Most Epic Off-Road Trails

As an avid off-roader, there's nothing quite like the thrill of hitting the trails and pushing your vehicle to its limits. Whether you're a seasoned pro or just starting out, the United States has no shortage of incredible off-road trails to explore. From the rocky mountains of Colorado to the sandy dunes of California, each trail offers a unique challenge and breathtaking scenery.

## History/Context

The art of off-roading has been around for decades, with early enthusiasts using modified vehicles to tackle rugged terrain. Over the years, the sport has evolved, with advances in technology and vehicle design making it more accessible to a wider range of people. Today, off-roading is a popular pastime, with thousands of trails across the U.S. and a thriving community of enthusiasts. The 'Best of On the Trail' 2021 series highlights 12 of the most iconic and challenging trails in the country, showcasing the diversity and beauty of the American landscape.

## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

When it comes to tackling off-road trails, having the right vehicle is crucial. A 4x4 with a capable engine, robust suspension, and ample ground clearance is essential for navigating rocky, steep, and uneven terrain. Some of the key technical details to consider when choosing an off-road vehicle include:

| Vehicle | Engine | Suspension | Ground Clearance |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Jeep Wrangler | 3.6L V6 | Coil-over shocks | 10.8 inches |
| Toyota 4Runner | 4.0L V6 | Coil springs | 9.6 inches |
| Ford Bronco | 2.3L EcoBoost | Independent suspension | 11.6 inches |
| Chevrolet Colorado | 3.6L V6 | Coil-over shocks | 8.4 inches |
| Land Rover Defender | 3.0L I6 | Air suspension | 11.6 inches |

Each of these vehicles has its strengths and weaknesses, and the right choice will depend on your specific needs and preferences.

## Real World Performance

So, how do these vehicles perform on the trails? We put them to the test on some of the toughest off-road trails in the U.S., including the infamous Rubicon Trail in California and the rugged trails of Moab, Utah. The results were impressive, with each vehicle showcasing its unique capabilities and handling characteristics. The Jeep Wrangler, with its legendary off-road prowess, proved to be a standout performer, while the Toyota 4Runner surprised us with its agility and traction.

## Comparison/Verdict

So, which off-road trail is the best? The answer, of course, depends on your personal preferences and skill level. If you're a beginner, you may want to start with a more gentle trail, such as the scenic trails of Big Bear, California. More experienced off-roaders, on the other hand, may prefer the challenging trails of Colorado or the rocky terrain of Arizona. Ultimately, the best off-road trail is one that challenges you, inspires you, and leaves you wanting more.

### FAQ

1.

**What is the most difficult off-road trail in the U.S.?**
The most difficult off-road trail in the U.S.

is often debated among enthusiasts, but the Rubicon Trail in California is widely considered one of the toughest.
2.

**What vehicle is best for off-roading?**
The best vehicle for off-roading depends on your specific needs and preferences, but popular choices include the Jeep Wrangler, Toyota 4Runner, and Ford Bronco.
3.

**Do I need a 4x4 to go off-roading?**
While a 4x4 is not strictly necessary for off-roading, it is highly recommended, as it provides the traction and stability needed to navigate challenging terrain.
4.

**What safety precautions should I take when off-roading?**
When off-roading, it's essential to take safety precautions, such as wearing a seatbelt, carrying a first aid kit, and informing someone of your itinerary and expected return time.
