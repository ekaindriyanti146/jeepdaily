## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Jeep Wrangler is renowned for its exceptional off-road capabilities, thanks to its robust engine and suspension system.

The vehicle features a 3.6-liter V6 engine, producing 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque.

The Wrangler's suspension system is designed to handle challenging terrain, with a five-link coil suspension setup in the front and a five-link leaf spring suspension in the rear.

The vehicle's ground clearance and approach/departure angles also contribute to its impressive off-road performance.

Here is a detailed breakdown of the Jeep Wrangler's technical specifications:
| Feature | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Engine | 3.6-liter V6 |
| Horsepower | 285 hp |
| Torque | 260 lb-ft |
| Suspension | Five-link coil suspension (front), five-link leaf spring suspension (rear) |
| Ground Clearance | 10.9 inches |
| Approach Angle | 34.6 degrees |
| Departure Angle | 29.2 degrees |
