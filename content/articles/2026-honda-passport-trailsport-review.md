# Introduction to the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport

The 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport is the latest iteration of Honda's mid-size SUV, boasting a rugged design, advanced all-wheel-drive system, and a plethora of off-road features. As a senior automotive journalist, I had the opportunity to put this vehicle to the test, pushing its limits on some of the toughest trails in the country. In this comprehensive review, we'll delve into the history of the Passport, its technical details, real-world performance, and how it stacks up against the competition.

## History and Context of the Honda Passport

The Honda Passport has a rich history, dating back to the 1990s. Initially, it was a rebadged Isuzu Rodeo, but in 2019, Honda reintroduced the Passport as a mid-size SUV, built on the same platform as the Pilot. The new Passport was designed to fill the gap between the CR-V and Pilot, offering a more rugged and off-road-capable vehicle. The TrailSport trim is the latest addition to the Passport lineup, featuring unique exterior styling, upgraded suspension, and advanced all-wheel-drive technology.

## Technical Details, Engine, and Suspension

The 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine, producing 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission and Honda's advanced all-wheel-drive system, which features a torque-vectoring rear differential. The TrailSport trim also boasts a unique suspension setup, with increased ground clearance, heavier-duty springs, and shocks tuned for off-road use. The vehicle's approach and departure angles have also been improved, making it more capable on steep inclines and declines.

## Real-World Performance of the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport

During my testing, the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport proved to be an exceptional off-road performer. The vehicle's advanced all-wheel-drive system and torque-vectoring rear differential worked seamlessly together, providing traction and stability on a variety of terrain, from rocky trails to sandy dunes. The unique suspension setup also helped to absorb bumps and shocks, making the ride surprisingly smooth and comfortable. On the road, the Passport TrailSport handled well, with responsive steering and ample power from the V6 engine.

## Comparison and Verdict

The 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport is an excellent choice for those seeking a mid-size SUV with exceptional off-road capability. While it may not be as rugged as some of its competitors, such as the Jeep Grand Cherokee, it offers a more refined and comfortable on-road experience. In comparison to the Toyota 4Runner, the Passport TrailSport boasts more advanced technology and a more modern design. Ultimately, the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport is a well-rounded vehicle that excels in both on-road and off-road environments.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1.

**What is the towing capacity of the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport?**
The towing capacity of the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport is up to 5,000 pounds, making it suitable for towing small boats, trailers, and RVs.
2.

**Does the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport come with a locking rear differential?**
No, the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport does not come with a locking rear differential.

Instead, it features a torque-vectoring rear differential that helps to improve traction and stability on various terrain.
3.

**Is the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport available with a manual transmission?**
No, the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport is only available with a 9-speed automatic transmission.
4.

**What is the starting price of the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport?**
The starting price of the 2026 Honda Passport TrailSport is around $43,000, making it a competitive option in the mid-size SUV market.
