## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Jeep Gladiator is available with two engine options: a 3.6-liter V6 and a 3.0-liter EcoDiesel V6.

The V6 engine produces 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, while the EcoDiesel engine produces 260 horsepower and 442 lb-ft of torque.

The Gladiator features a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link leaf spring suspension in the rear, providing a smooth and comfortable ride both on and off the road.

The vehicle's technical details are as follows:
| **Feature** | **Description** |
| --- | --- |
| Engine | 3.6-liter V6 or 3.0-liter EcoDiesel V6 |
| Horsepower | 285 hp (V6) or 260 hp (EcoDiesel) |
| Torque | 260 lb-ft (V6) or 442 lb-ft (EcoDiesel) |
| Suspension | Five-link coil (front) and five-link leaf spring (rear) |
| Transmission | 6-speed manual or 8-speed automatic |
| Drive Type | Four-wheel drive |

## Section 3: Real World Performance

The Jeep Gladiator's real-world performance is impressive, with its powerful engine and capable suspension system making it an excellent choice for both on-road and off-road driving. The vehicle's towing capacity is up to 7,650 pounds, and its payload capacity is up to 1,700 pounds. The Gladiator's fuel economy is also respectable, with an estimated 17 mpg in the city and 23 mpg on the highway. Overall, the Gladiator's performance, capability, and features make it an excellent choice for those seeking a reliable and versatile midsize pickup truck.
