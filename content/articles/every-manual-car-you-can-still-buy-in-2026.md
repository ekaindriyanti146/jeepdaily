## Hook

As we dive into the year 2026, the automotive landscape continues to evolve, with a significant shift towards automatic and semi-automatic transmissions.

However, for the driving purists, there's still a glimmer of hope.

Many manufacturers, including BMW, Cadillac, Honda, and more, continue to offer manual transmission options in their lineup.

In this article, we'll delve into the world of manual cars, exploring the history, technical details, and real-world performance of these vehicles.

Whether you're a seasoned enthusiast or a newcomer to the world of manual transmissions, this comprehensive guide will provide you with an in-depth look at every manual car you can still buy in 2026.

## Section 1: History/Context

The manual transmission has been a staple of the automotive world for decades.

With its roots dating back to the early 20th century, the manual transmission has undergone significant transformations over the years.

From the early days of non-synchromesh transmissions to the modern dual-clutch transmissions, the technology has evolved to provide smoother, more precise shifting.

Despite the rise of automatic transmissions, many manufacturers continue to offer manual options, catering to the needs of driving enthusiasts.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

When it comes to manual transmissions, the technical details play a crucial role in determining the overall driving experience.

The type of transmission, engine, and suspension all work in harmony to provide a seamless driving experience.

For instance, the BMW M3 features a 6-speed manual transmission, paired with a 3.0L inline-6 engine, producing 473 horsepower.

On the other hand, the Honda Civic Si boasts a 6-speed manual transmission, paired with a 1.5L turbocharged engine, producing 205 horsepower.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

In the real world, the performance of manual cars is just as impressive as their technical specifications.

The BMW M3, for instance, can go from 0-60mph in just 4.1 seconds, while the Honda Civic Si can achieve the same feat in 6.7 seconds.

The Cadillac CT4-V, on the other hand, can go from 0-60mph in 4.8 seconds.

The Subaru WRX and Toyota 86 also deliver impressive performance, with 0-60mph times of 5.4 seconds and 6.4 seconds, respectively.

Whether you're cruising down the highway or carving through twisty roads, manual cars offer a level of engagement and excitement that's hard to match with automatic transmissions.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

When comparing the manual cars available in 2026, it's clear that each model has its own unique strengths and weaknesses.

The BMW M3, for instance, is a high-performance powerhouse, while the Honda Civic Si is a more affordable, yet still exciting, option.

The Cadillac CT4-V, on the other hand, offers a luxurious interior and impressive performance.

Ultimately, the choice of manual car will depend on your individual needs and preferences.

If you're looking for a high-performance driving experience, the BMW M3 or Cadillac CT4-V may be the way to go.

However, if you're on a budget, the Honda Civic Si or Subaru WRX may be a more affordable option.
