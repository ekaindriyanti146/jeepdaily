# Why the Rubicon Dominates the Rocks
The Jeep brand has been synonymous with off-road adventure for decades, and its flagship model, the Rubicon, is the epitome of this spirit. With its rich history dating back to the CJ model code, Jeep has continually pushed the boundaries of what is possible in off-road exploration. The YJ, TJ, JK, JL, and JT model codes have each contributed to the evolution of the Jeep, culminating in the current JL and JT models that boast unparalleled off-road capabilities.

## The Pentastar V6 Reliability Verdict
At the heart of the Jeep's off-road prowess is its powerful engine. The Pentastar V6, with its 3.6 liters of displacement, produces 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. For those seeking even more power, the Hemi V8 is an option, delivering 470 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque. The 4xe hybrid powertrain offers a unique blend of efficiency and performance, making it an attractive option for those who want to conquer the trails while minimizing their environmental footprint.

## Towing Capacity and Performance
The Jeep's towing capacity is impressive, with the ability to tow up to 3,500 pounds with the Pentastar V6 engine. The Hemi V8 increases this capacity to 5,000 pounds, making it an excellent choice for those who need to tow trailers or boats. The 4xe hybrid powertrain offers a towing capacity of up to 3,500 pounds, providing a balance between performance and efficiency.
