## History/Context

The Subaru Outback has a long history dating back to the 1990s, when it was first introduced as a more rugged version of the Subaru Legacy wagon.

Over the years, the Outback has undergone several transformations, with each new generation bringing improved off-road capabilities and more refined on-road manners.

With its increased ground clearance, improved approach and departure angles, and more aggressive tires, the Outback Wilderness is well-suited for off-road excursions.

## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Subaru Outback Wilderness is powered by a 2.4-liter turbocharged boxer engine, which produces 260 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque.

The engine is paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT) and Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel-drive system, which provides excellent traction and control on various surfaces.

The suspension has been upgraded with more robust components, including thicker anti-roll bars and heavier-duty springs, to improve stability and reduce body roll.

The Australian version of the Outback Wilderness differs from the US model in one key way: it will not feature the dual-function X-Mode system, which allows drivers to switch between two different modes for tackling challenging terrain.

## Real World Performance

In real-world testing, the Subaru Outback Wilderness has proven to be a capable off-road vehicle, with its aggressive tires and improved suspension allowing it to tackle challenging terrain with ease. The 2.4-liter turbocharged engine provides plenty of power for merging onto highways or climbing steep hills, and the CVT transmission does an excellent job of keeping the engine in its power band. While the lack of a dual-function X-Mode system in the Australian model may be a disappointment for some, the single-function system still provides excellent traction and control on various surfaces.

## Comparison/Verdict

Compared to other off-road vehicles in its class, the Subaru Outback Wilderness is a strong contender, offering a unique blend of on-road refinement and off-road capability. While the Australian model may not feature the dual-function X-Mode system, it still provides excellent performance and value for adventurous buyers. Ultimately, the decision to purchase an Outback Wilderness will depend on individual preferences and priorities. For those who value a comfortable and capable off-road vehicle, the Subaru Outback Wilderness is definitely worth considering.
