## Hook

The 2026 Jeep Cherokee has been a subject of interest for off-road enthusiasts, with its promises of improved performance and capabilities. However, a recent review by TFLcar has revealed that the vehicle seriously struggles off-road. This has left many wondering if the Cherokee can truly deliver on its promises. In this article, we will delve into the history of the Jeep Cherokee, its technical details, and real-world performance to understand where it falls short.

## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Cherokee has a long history of being a capable off-road vehicle, with its roots dating back to the 1970s. Over the years, the Cherokee has undergone numerous redesigns and updates, with each iteration aiming to improve its performance and capabilities. The current generation of the Cherokee, which was introduced in 2014, has been praised for its on-road comfort and features, but its off-road performance has been a subject of debate. The 2026 model was expected to address these concerns, but it seems that it still has a long way to go.

The Cherokee's off-road struggles can be attributed to its unibody construction, which, while providing a more comfortable on-road experience, compromises its ability to withstand the rigors of off-road driving. Additionally, the vehicle's weight distribution and ground clearance have been criticized for being inadequate for serious off-roading. Despite these limitations, the Cherokee remains a popular choice for those who want a vehicle that can handle both on-road and off-road duties.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The 2026 Jeep Cherokee comes with a range of engine options, including a 2.4-liter inline-four and a 3.2-liter V6. The vehicle also features a number of advanced technologies, such as a rear axle disconnect and a Selec-Terrain traction management system. However, these features are not enough to overcome the Cherokee's fundamental limitations.

The Cherokee's suspension is another area of concern, with its front and rear independent suspension struggling to cope with the demands of off-road driving. The vehicle's approach and departure angles are also inadequate, making it prone to scraping and damage when navigating rocky or uneven terrain.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

In real-world testing, the 2026 Jeep Cherokee has been found to struggle with even moderate off-road terrain. The vehicle's lack of ground clearance and inadequate approach and departure angles make it prone to scraping and damage, while its suspension struggles to cope with the demands of off-road driving.

The Cherokee's engine and transmission also struggle to provide the necessary power and torque for off-road driving, making it difficult to navigate steep inclines or rocky terrain. While the vehicle's advanced technologies, such as the Selec-Terrain traction management system, can help to improve its performance, they are not enough to overcome its fundamental limitations.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

In comparison to other vehicles in its class, the 2026 Jeep Cherokee falls short in terms of off-road performance. The Toyota 4Runner and the Jeep Wrangler, for example, offer more capable off-road performances, thanks to their body-on-frame construction and more advanced suspension systems.

While the Cherokee may not be the best choice for serious off-roaders, it remains a capable and comfortable on-road vehicle. However, for those who want a vehicle that can handle both on-road and off-road duties, there may be better options available.
