## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

When it comes to off-road performance, the technical details of a vehicle play a crucial role.

Jeep's Wrangler, for instance, boasts a robust 3.6L V6 engine that produces 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque.

The Wrangler also features a capable suspension system, including a disconnecting front sway bar and a heavy-duty rear axle.

The Grand Cherokee, on the other hand, offers a more refined on-road experience while still maintaining its off-road prowess.

Chrysler's vehicles, such as the Pacifica, may not be designed for extreme off-roading, but they offer a comfortable and feature-rich experience for those who prioritize luxury over adventure.
