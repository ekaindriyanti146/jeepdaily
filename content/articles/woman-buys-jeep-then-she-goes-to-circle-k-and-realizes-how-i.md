## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Jeep Wrangler, for example, comes with a variety of engine options, including a 3.6-liter V6 engine and a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine. The 3.6-liter V6 engine produces 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces 270 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The Wrangler also features a suspension system that includes a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil suspension with a track bar in the rear. While these features contribute to the Jeep's off-road capabilities, they also impact fuel efficiency.
