However, the recall of over 24,000 hybrid Jeep Wranglers due to a flawed software update raises concerns about the reliability and safety of these vehicles.

## Technical Details of the Recall

The recall is attributed to a flawed software update that affects the vehicle's powertrain control module. The software update was intended to improve the vehicle's performance and efficiency, but it has been found to cause issues with the transmission and engine. The technical details of the recall are as follows:

| **Component** | **Description** | **Failure Mode** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Powertrain Control Module | Controls the vehicle's engine and transmission | Flawed software update causing transmission and engine issues |
| Transmission | 8-speed automatic | Slipping or hesitation due to software update |
| Engine | 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 | Reduced power output or stalling due to software update |

The recall affects certain model year 2020 and 2021 hybrid Jeep Wranglers, and FCA US will notify owners and provide a free software update to rectify the issue.

## Real-World Performance of the Affected Vehicles

The real-world performance of the affected vehicles has been reported to be inconsistent, with some owners experiencing issues with the transmission and engine, while others have not reported any problems.
