## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The recall affects certain models of Jeep's Grand Cherokee and Wrangler vehicles, which are equipped with a faulty fuel pump that can cause a fire. The fuel pump is located inside the fuel tank, and can overheat, causing a fire that can spread quickly. The recall is expected to cost Jeep millions of dollars, and will require the replacement of the faulty fuel pumps in all affected vehicles. The table below outlines the technical details of the recall:

| Model | Year | Engine | Suspension | Fuel Pump |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Grand Cherokee | 2018-2020 | 3.6L V6 | Quadra-Trac II | Faulty |
| Wrangler | 2018-2020 | 3.6L V6 | Rock-Trac | Faulty |
| Grand Cherokee | 2020-2022 | 5.7L V8 | Quadra-Trac II | Not affected |
| Wrangler | 2020-2022 | 3.6L V6 | Rock-Trac | Not affected |
