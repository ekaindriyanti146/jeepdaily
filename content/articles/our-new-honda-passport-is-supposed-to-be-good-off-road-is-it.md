# Introduction

The Honda Passport is a mid-size SUV that has been making waves in the automotive world with its impressive off-road capabilities. But is it really as good as everyone says it is? In this article, we'll take a closer look at the Passport's history, technical details, and real-world performance to find out.

## Section 1: History/Context

The Honda Passport has been around since the 1990s, but it wasn't until the latest generation that it started to gain attention for its off-road prowess. The current model was introduced in 2019 and has since become a favorite among outdoor enthusiasts. With its rugged design and impressive ground clearance, the Passport is well-equipped to handle tough terrain. But what sets it apart from other SUVs in its class?

The Passport's off-road capabilities can be attributed to its intelligent Variable Torque Management (i-VTM4) all-wheel-drive system, which allows for seamless transitions between front-wheel drive and all-wheel drive. This system, combined with the Passport's robust suspension and generous approach and departure angles, makes it an excellent choice for those who want to venture off the beaten path.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

Under the hood, the Passport is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission that provides smooth and responsive shifting. The Passport also features a robust suspension system, including a MacPherson strut front suspension and a multi-link rear suspension, which helps to absorb bumps and maintain stability on uneven terrain.

| **Feature** | **Description** |
| --- | --- |
| Engine | 3.5-liter V6 |
| Horsepower | 280 |
| Torque | 262 lb-ft |
| Transmission | 9-speed automatic |
| Drive Type | Intelligent Variable Torque Management (i-VTM4) all-wheel drive |
| Suspension | MacPherson strut front, multi-link rear |
| Ground Clearance | 7.5 inches |
| Approach Angle | 21.4 degrees |
| Departure Angle | 20.6 degrees |

The Passport's intelligent all-wheel-drive system is capable of sending up to 70% of the engine's torque to the rear wheels, and up to 100% of that torque can be sent to either the left or right rear wheel. This allows for improved traction and control on slippery or uneven surfaces.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

So how does the Passport perform in real-world off-road situations? We took the Passport on a variety of trails, from smooth dirt roads to rocky and rutted paths. The results were impressive, with the Passport handling each obstacle with ease. The intelligent all-wheel-drive system worked seamlessly, providing traction and control on even the most challenging terrain.

The Passport's suspension also proved to be up to the task, absorbing bumps and maintaining stability on uneven surfaces. The engine provided plenty of power, allowing us to climb steep inclines and navigate rocky terrain with confidence.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

So how does the Honda Passport stack up against other mid-size SUVs in its class? In terms of off-road capability, the Passport is certainly one of the best. Its intelligent all-wheel-drive system and robust suspension make it an excellent choice for those who want to venture off the beaten path.

However, the Passport may not be the best choice for those who prioritize on-road comfort and handling. While it is certainly capable on paved roads, the Passport's suspension can feel a bit stiff and its engine can be noisy at high speeds.

Overall, the Honda Passport is an excellent choice for those who want a capable off-road SUV. With its intelligent all-wheel-drive system, robust suspension, and generous ground clearance, the Passport is well-equipped to handle even the toughest terrain.
