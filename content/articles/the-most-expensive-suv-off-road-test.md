The most expensive SUVs on the market boast some of the most advanced 4x4 systems, engines, and suspension setups available.

The Rolls-Royce Cullinan, for example, features a 6.6-liter V12 engine producing 563 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque.

The Cullinan also boasts a advanced all-wheel-drive system with a two-speed transfer case and a wade-sensing system that can detect water depth.

The Lamborghini Urus, on the other hand, features a 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 engine producing 641 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque.

The Urus also boasts a advanced all-wheel-drive system with a torque-vectoring rear differential and a central torsen differential.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

So how do these vehicles perform in real-world off-road conditions? To find out, we took the Rolls-Royce Cullinan, Lamborghini Urus, Bentley Bentayga, and Range Rover SVAutobiography on a challenging off-road course.

The course featured steep inclines, rocky terrain, and water crossings.

The Rolls-Royce Cullinan impressed with its ability to tackle steep inclines and rocky terrain with ease, thanks to its advanced all-wheel-drive system and high-ground clearance.

The Lamborghini Urus, on the other hand, was a revelation on the rocky terrain, with its torque-vectoring rear differential and adaptive air suspension system allowing it to handle tight corners and uneven terrain with precision.

The Bentley Bentayga and Range Rover SVAutobiography also performed well, but were slightly held back by their less advanced 4x4 systems.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

So which of these vehicles is the best off-road performer? The answer depends on what you are looking for.

If you want a vehicle that can handle steep inclines and rocky terrain with ease, the Rolls-Royce Cullinan is the clear winner.

If you want a vehicle that can handle tight corners and uneven terrain with precision, the Lamborghini Urus is the way to go.

The Bentley Bentayga and Range Rover SVAutobiography are also capable off-road performers, but are slightly held back by their less advanced 4x4 systems.
