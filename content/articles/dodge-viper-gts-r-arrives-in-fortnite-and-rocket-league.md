## Section 1: History/Context

The Dodge Viper GTS-R has a rich history in the world of motorsports, with numerous victories and accolades to its name. First introduced in the 1990s, the Viper GTS-R was designed to compete in various racing series, including the 24 Hours of Le Mans. Its impressive performance and handling capabilities made it a favorite among racing teams and drivers. The Viper GTS-R's success on the track has cemented its status as an iconic American muscle car.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Dodge Viper GTS-R is powered by a massive 8.0-liter V10 engine, producing an astonishing 700 horsepower and 645 lb-ft of torque. This potent engine is paired with a 6-speed manual transmission, allowing drivers to harness the car's incredible power. The Viper GTS-R's suspension system features a double-wishbone setup with coil springs and anti-roll bars, providing exceptional handling and stability. The car's aerodynamics have been carefully crafted to reduce drag and enhance downforce, making it a formidable competitor on the track.
