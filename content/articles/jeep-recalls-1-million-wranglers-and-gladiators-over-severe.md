## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator have a long and storied history, dating back to the 1940s. The Wrangler, in particular, has been a staple of the Jeep brand, known for its ruggedness, reliability, and off-road capabilities. The Gladiator, on the other hand, is a relatively new addition to the Jeep family, having been introduced in 2019. Despite their differences, both vehicles have been praised for their performance, style, and versatility. However, the recent recall has cast a shadow over the reputation of these vehicles, highlighting the need for increased scrutiny and quality control.

The recall is attributed to an electrical issue, which can cause a fire in the vehicle's engine compartment. This is a significant concern, as it poses a risk not only to the vehicle but also to the occupants and surrounding environment. Jeep has stated that the recall is precautionary and that they are working to rectify the issue as quickly as possible.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The technical details surrounding the recall are complex and multifaceted. The issue is attributed to a faulty electrical connection in the vehicle's engine compartment, which can cause a short circuit and subsequent fire. The recall affects certain model year Wranglers and Gladiators, equipped with specific engine and transmission combinations.

| Gladiator | 2020-2022 | 3.6L V6 | 6-speed manual |
