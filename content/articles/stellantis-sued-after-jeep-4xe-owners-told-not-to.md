# Stellantis Sued After Jeep 4xe Owners Told Not to Charge Their SUVs

## Introduction to the Issue
The automotive industry has been rocked by a recent lawsuit filed against Stellantis, the parent company of Jeep, after owners of the Jeep 4xe were instructed not to charge their vehicles due to a potential fire risk. This issue has raised concerns among electric vehicle owners and has sparked a wider conversation about the safety and reliability of electric vehicles.

## The Problem with the Jeep 4xe
The Jeep 4xe is a plug-in hybrid electric vehicle that has been marketed as a more environmentally friendly alternative to traditional gasoline-powered vehicles. However, recent reports have suggested that the vehicle's battery may be prone to overheating, which can increase the risk of a fire. As a result, Stellantis has instructed owners not to charge their vehicles, which has caused frustration and inconvenience for many owners.

### Features of the Jeep 4xe
Some of the key features of the Jeep 4xe include:
* Electric motor with a battery pack
* Plug-in hybrid capability
* Four-wheel drive system
* Advanced safety features, including forward collision warning and adaptive cruise control

## The Lawsuit Against Stellantis
The class-action lawsuit filed against Stellantis alleges that the company knew about the potential fire risk associated with the Jeep 4xe, but failed to disclose this information to owners. The lawsuit also claims that Stellantis has not provided adequate solutions to address the issue, leaving owners with vehicles that they cannot use as intended.
