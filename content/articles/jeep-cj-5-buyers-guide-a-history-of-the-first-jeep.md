# Introduction to the Jeep CJ-5
The Jeep CJ-5 is a legendary off-road vehicle that has been a favorite among adventure-seekers and collectors alike. First introduced in 1955, the CJ-5 was the first civilian version of the military Jeep, designed for the masses. In this guide, we'll delve into the history of the CJ-5, its features, and what to look for when buying one.

## History of the Jeep CJ-5
The CJ-5 was the first Jeep to feature the now-iconic 'funny fender' design, with rounded fenders and a more curved body style. This design change was made to accommodate larger tires and to give the Jeep a more aggressive stance. The CJ-5 was produced from 1955 to 1983, with various engine options and transmission types available.

### Models and Variations
Over the years, the CJ-5 underwent several changes and updates. Some notable models and variations include:
* CJ-5 (1955-1975): The original CJ-5, featuring a 3-speed manual transmission and a 134 cubic inch inline-4 engine.
* CJ-5A (1955-1964): A variant of the CJ-5 with a 4-speed manual transmission and a 161 cubic inch inline-6 engine.
* CJ-5B (1965-1971): A updated version of the CJ-5 with a more powerful 232 cubic inch inline-6 engine.

## Features and Specifications
The Jeep CJ-5 was known for its ruggedness and off-road capability. Some notable features and specifications include:
* Engine: 134 cubic inch inline-4, 161 cubic inch inline-6, or 232 cubic inch inline-6
* Transmission: 3-speed manual or 4-speed manual
* Drive Type: Four-wheel drive
* Suspension: Leaf spring suspension with solid axles
* Brakes: Drum brakes on all four wheels
* Tires: 6.00-16 or 7.00-16

### Buying a Jeep CJ-5
If you're in the market for a Jeep CJ-5, there are several things to look for. Here are some key features to consider:
* Condition: Look for signs of rust, damage, or wear on the body and frame.
* Engine: Check the engine for any signs of leaks, damage, or excessive wear.
* Transmission: Check the transmission for any signs of damage or wear.
* Suspension: Check the suspension for any signs of wear or damage.
* Brakes: Check the brakes for any signs of wear or damage.
* Electrical: Check the electrical system for any signs of damage or wear.

Some key features to look for when buying a Jeep CJ-5 include:
* Original engine and transmission
* Original suspension and brakes
* Original body and frame
* No signs of rust or damage
* Clean title and documentation
