The Jeep Wrangler is an iconic off-road vehicle that has been a staple of the automotive industry for decades. ## Section 1: History and Context of the Jeep Wrangler

The Jeep Wrangler has a long and storied history that dates back to the 1940s. The first Jeep, known as the Willys MB, was produced during World War II and was designed for military use. After the war, the Willys MB was adapted for civilian use and became known as the CJ-2. Over the years, the Jeep Wrangler has undergone numerous design changes, updates, and improvements, but its core philosophy has remained the same: to provide a rugged, reliable, and capable off-road vehicle.

The Jeep Wrangler has been produced in several different generations, each with its own unique characteristics and features.

The first generation, known as the YJ, was produced from 1986 to 1995 and featured a rectangular headlights and a more angular design.
