## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler has a long and storied history that dates back to the 1940s.

The first Jeep, the Willys MB, was created during World War II as a military vehicle.

The Willys MB was designed to be a rugged, reliable, and versatile vehicle that could handle the toughest terrain.

After the war, the Willys MB was adapted for civilian use and became known as the CJ-2.

The CJ-2 was the first civilian Jeep and paved the way for the modern Wrangler.

Over the years, the Wrangler has undergone many changes and updates, but its core mission has remained the same: to provide a rugged, reliable, and versatile vehicle for off-road enthusiasts.
