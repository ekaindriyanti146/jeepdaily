### FAQ

1. **Q: Are lifted Subarus more prone to rollovers?**
A: While lifting a vehicle can increase its center of gravity, the risk of rollover can be mitigated with proper suspension upgrades and safe driving practices.

2. **Q: Can I lift my Subaru myself, or do I need a professional?**
A: While DIY kits are available, lifting a vehicle is a complex process that requires expertise to ensure safety and performance. It's highly recommended to consult a professional.

3. **Q: How much does it cost to lift a Subaru?**
A: The cost can vary widely depending on the model, lift height, and other modifications. On average, a lift kit can cost between $1,000 to $5,000, not including installation and other upgrades.

4. **Q: Do lifted Subarus require special maintenance?**
A: Yes, lifted vehicles may require more frequent checks on suspension components and alignment due to the increased stress on these parts. Regular maintenance is crucial to ensure the longevity and performance of the vehicle.
