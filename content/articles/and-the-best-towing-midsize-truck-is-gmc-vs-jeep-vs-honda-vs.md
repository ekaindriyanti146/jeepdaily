The GMC Canyon, Jeep Gladiator, and Honda Ridgeline are three of the most popular midsize trucks on the market, each with its unique strengths and weaknesses.

The Ike Gauntlet, a notorious towing test, will push these trucks to their limits, revealing which one is the ultimate towing champion.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The GMC Canyon is powered by a 3.6L V6 engine, producing 308 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque.

It features a durable suspension system, including a coil-over-shock front suspension and a live axle rear suspension with a two-stage multi-leaf spring.

The Jeep Gladiator, on the other hand, boasts a 3.6L V6 engine, generating 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque.

Its suspension system includes a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link leaf spring suspension in the rear.

The Honda Ridgeline is equipped with a 3.5L V6 engine, producing 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque.

Its suspension system features a MacPherson strut front suspension and a multi-link rear suspension with a trailing arm.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

The Ike Gauntlet is a grueling test that pushes trucks to their limits, simulating real-world towing scenarios. The test consists of a steep incline, followed by a decline, and finally, a series of twisties. The GMC Canyon performed admirably, its powerful engine and durable suspension system allowing it to tackle the course with ease. The Jeep Gladiator, with its impressive ground clearance and articulation, also handled the test with confidence. The Honda Ridgeline, however, struggled with the incline, its engine lacking the necessary oomph to propel it up the steep grade.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

After putting the GMC Canyon, Jeep Gladiator, and Honda Ridgeline through the Ike Gauntlet, it's clear that the GMC Canyon is the best midsize truck for towing. Its powerful engine, durable suspension system, and impressive capabilities make it the ultimate towing champion. The Jeep Gladiator comes in second, its impressive ground clearance and articulation making it a close contender. The Honda Ridgeline, while a capable truck in its own right, falls short in the towing department.
