## Section 1: History/Context

The Ford Ranger has a long and storied history, dating back to 1983 when the first generation of the vehicle was introduced.

Initially designed as a compact pickup truck, the Ranger quickly gained popularity for its reliability, durability, and off-road prowess.

Over the years, the Ranger has undergone numerous updates and redesigns, with the current generation being the most advanced and capable to date.

The Wildtrak trim, in particular, is designed for serious off-road enthusiasts, with features like a locking rear differential, hill descent control, and a terrain management system.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

Under the hood of the Ford Ranger Wildtrak lies a powerful 2.0-liter bi-turbo diesel engine, producing 213 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque.

This robust powerplant is paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission, which provides smooth and seamless shifting even in the most challenging off-road conditions.

The Wildtrak's suspension system is equally impressive, with a double-wishbone setup in the front and a leaf-spring configuration in the rear.

This combination provides excellent stability and articulation, allowing the vehicle to navigate rocky, uneven terrain with ease.

Other notable features include a wade-sensing system, which can detect water depth and alert the driver to potential hazards, and a comprehensive suite of safety features like trailer sway control and blind-spot monitoring.

During my time behind the wheel of the Ford Ranger Wildtrak, I was consistently impressed by its off-road capabilities.

### FAQ

1.

**What is the Ford Ranger Wildtrak's towing capacity?**
The Ford Ranger Wildtrak has a maximum towing capacity of up to 3,500kg, depending on the specific trim level and configuration.
2.

**Can the Ford Ranger Wildtrak handle water crossings?**
Yes, the Ford Ranger Wildtrak features a wade-sensing system that can detect water depth and alert the driver to potential hazards.

The vehicle can handle water crossings up to 800mm deep.
3.

**What safety features does the Ford Ranger Wildtrak offer?**
The Ford Ranger Wildtrak offers a comprehensive suite of safety features, including trailer sway control, blind-spot monitoring, and lane departure warning.
4.

**Is the Ford Ranger Wildtrak available with a manual transmission?**
No, the Ford Ranger Wildtrak is only available with a 10-speed automatic transmission.
