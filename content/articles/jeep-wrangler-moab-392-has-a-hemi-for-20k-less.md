Over the years, the Wrangler has evolved to become a iconic symbol of off-road capability and adventure. The Moab 392 is the latest iteration of this legacy, named after the infamous Moab off-road trail in Utah. This vehicle is designed to pay homage to the Wrangler's heritage while incorporating modern technology and engineering. The Moab 392 is built on the same platform as the standard Wrangler, but with significant upgrades to the engine, suspension, and interior.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Moab 392 is powered by a 6.4-liter Hemi V8 engine, which produces 470 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque.

This engine is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission and a two-speed transfer case, allowing for seamless shifting between two-wheel and four-wheel drive.

The suspension has been upgraded to include FOX shocks, which provide improved damping and stability on uneven terrain.

The Moab 392 also features a unique hood design, which allows for increased clearance and improved airflow to the engine.

The vehicle's interior has been upgraded with premium materials, including leather-trimmed seats and a premium audio system.
