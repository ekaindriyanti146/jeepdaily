## The Birth of a Legend: Willys and the CJ Series
The Jeep story begins with the Willys-Overland Motor Company, which produced the first civilian Jeep, the CJ-2, in 1945. The CJ series, including the CJ-3, CJ-5, and CJ-7, would go on to become a staple of the Jeep brand, known for their ruggedness and off-road capability. The YJ, introduced in 1986, marked a significant departure from the traditional CJ design, with a more modern and aerodynamic look.
## The Wrangler's 4:1 Transfer Case Explained
The TJ, launched in 1996, saw the return of the round headlights, a design element that has become synonymous with the Jeep Wrangler. The TJ also introduced a new 4:1 transfer case, which provided improved low-range gearing for off-road enthusiasts. The JK, which debuted in 2006, brought significant updates, including a new engine and an improved suspension system. The current JL and JT models continue this legacy, offering unparalleled off-road capability and on-road refinement.

## The Evolution of the Wrangler
From its humble beginnings as a military vehicle to its current status as an off-road icon, the Jeep Wrangler has come a long way. With its removable doors and top, the Wrangler offers an unparalleled open-air driving experience. The Rubicon trim, introduced in 2002, takes the Wrangler's off-road capability to the next level, with features like locking differentials and a disconnecting front sway bar.
## A Legacy of Innovation
Throughout its 75-year history, Jeep has consistently pushed the boundaries of innovation, introducing new technologies and features that have helped shape the off-road landscape. From the Quadra-Trac system to the modern-day Selec-Trac system, Jeep has remained at the forefront of 4x4 technology. As the brand continues to evolve, one thing remains constant: the unwavering commitment to building vehicles that can tackle even the toughest terrain.
