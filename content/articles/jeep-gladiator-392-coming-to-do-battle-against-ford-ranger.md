The Jeep Gladiator has been around since 2019, but the 392 model is a relatively new addition to the lineup.

This truck is the result of a collaboration between Jeep and Mopar, the company's performance division.

The goal was to create a vehicle that would combine the Gladiator's impressive off-road capabilities with the power and performance of a V8 engine.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

So, what exactly makes the Jeep Gladiator 392 tick? Under the hood, you'll find a 6.4-liter V8 engine that produces 470 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission and a two-speed transfer case, giving the truck a maximum crawl ratio of 48:1. The suspension has been upgraded to handle the increased power, with a revised coil-spring setup and heavy-duty Fox shocks. The truck also features a range of other upgrades, including 33-inch all-terrain tires, a limited-slip rear differential, and a unique hood with a functional scoop.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

So, how does the Jeep Gladiator 392 perform in the real world? The answer is: extremely well. The truck's powerful V8 engine provides plenty of grunt, whether you're crawling over rocks or hauling heavy loads. The suspension is also impressive, providing a smooth ride on-road and plenty of articulation off-road. But what really sets the Gladiator 392 apart is its ability to handle high-speed off-road driving. With its powerful engine and capable suspension, this truck can tackle even the toughest trails with ease.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

So, how does the Jeep Gladiator 392 stack up against its main competitor, the Ford Ranger Raptor? The answer is: very well.

While the Ranger Raptor has a more advanced suspension setup and a higher top speed, the Gladiator 392 has a more powerful engine and a more refined on-road ride.

Ultimately, the choice between these two trucks will come down to personal preference.

If you're looking for a truck that can handle high-speed off-road driving and still provide a comfortable ride on-road, the Gladiator 392 may be the better choice.

But if you're looking for a truck with a more advanced suspension setup and a higher level of off-road capability, the Ranger Raptor may be the way to go.
