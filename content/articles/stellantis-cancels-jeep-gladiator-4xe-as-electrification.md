## History and Context of the Jeep Gladiator 4xe

The Jeep Gladiator 4xe was first unveiled in 2020 as part of Jeep's plan to electrify its lineup. The Gladiator 4xe was designed to combine the off-road capability of the traditional Gladiator with the efficiency of a plug-in hybrid electric vehicle. With its unique blend of power and sustainability, the Gladiator 4xe was poised to capture a significant share of the growing market for electric and hybrid off-road vehicles. However, it seems that Stellantis has decided to pivot its strategy, leaving the future of the Gladiator 4xe uncertain.

## Technical Details and Engine of the Jeep Gladiator 4xe

The Jeep Gladiator 4xe was equipped with a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine, paired with an electric motor and a 17-kilowatt-hour battery pack. This powertrain produced a combined 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque, making it one of the most capable off-road vehicles in its class. The Gladiator 4xe also featured a robust suspension system, including a disconnecting front sway bar and a heavy-duty Dana 44 rear axle. The technical details of the Gladiator 4xe were designed to provide unparalleled off-road performance, while also delivering improved fuel efficiency.
