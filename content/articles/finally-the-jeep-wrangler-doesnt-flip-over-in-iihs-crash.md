## Hook

The Jeep Wrangler, an iconic symbol of adventure and off-road capability, has faced criticism for its safety record, particularly in regards to rollover accidents.

However, after years of development and refinement, the Wrangler has finally achieved a significant milestone: it no longer flips over in IIHS crash tests.

This achievement marks a major turning point in the vehicle's history, as it demonstrates a commitment to safety without compromising its off-road prowess.

In this article, we will explore the history behind this achievement, the technical details that made it possible, and what it means for the future of the Jeep Wrangler.

## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler has a long and storied history, dating back to the Willys MB of World War II.

Over the years, the Wrangler has evolved significantly, with each new generation bringing improvements in design, capability, and safety.

However, despite these advancements, the Wrangler has consistently struggled with rollover accidents, which have led to criticism from safety advocates and regulatory bodies.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

So, what exactly has changed in the latest generation of the Jeep Wrangler to make it safer? One key factor is the use of high-strength steel in the vehicle's frame and body, which provides increased strength and rigidity.

Additionally, the Wrangler now features a more advanced airbag system, including side curtain airbags and a driver's knee airbag, which help to protect occupants in the event of a crash.

The vehicle's suspension system has also been revised, with the introduction of a new five-link coil suspension setup that provides improved stability and control.

Furthermore, the Wrangler now comes equipped with a range of electronic safety features, including electronic stability control, anti-lock braking, and traction control.

These features work together to help prevent accidents and reduce the risk of rollover.

But how does the latest generation of the Jeep Wrangler perform in real-world testing? The answer is: very well.

In recent IIHS crash tests, the Wrangler has demonstrated significant improvements in safety, including a reduced risk of rollover.

Additionally, the Wrangler has received positive reviews from owners and critics alike, who praise its improved on-road handling and off-road capability.
