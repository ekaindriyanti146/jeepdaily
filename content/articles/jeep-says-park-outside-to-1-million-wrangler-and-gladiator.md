## Hook

The Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator are two of the most iconic off-road vehicles on the market, known for their ruggedness and reliability. However, a recent recall notice has left many owners concerned about the safety of their vehicles. Jeep has issued a recall for approximately 1 million Wrangler and Gladiator vehicles due to a potential fire risk, advising owners to park their vehicles outside and away from structures until the issue is resolved. In this article, we will take a closer look at the recall, its causes, and what it means for owners.

## Section 1: History/Context

The recall affects certain model year 2018-2022 Wrangler and 2020-2022 Gladiator vehicles equipped with a 3.6L engine and an automatic transmission.

According to Jeep, the issue is related to an electrical short in the transmission that can cause a fire.

The company has reported several instances of fires related to this issue, although no injuries or fatalities have been reported.

The recall is a precautionary measure to ensure the safety of owners and their vehicles.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The recall is related to an electrical short in the transmission that can cause a fire. The short is believed to be caused by a faulty wiring harness that can overheat and ignite. The issue is more likely to occur when the vehicle is parked and the engine is turned off. Jeep has identified the root cause of the problem and is working on a solution to repair the affected vehicles. The repair will involve replacing the faulty wiring harness and updating the transmission software to prevent similar issues in the future. The following table provides more details on the affected vehicles:

| Model Year | Vehicle | Engine | Transmission |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 2018-2022 | Wrangler | 3.6L | Automatic |
| 2020-2022 | Gladiator | 3.6L | Automatic |

The recall highlights the importance of regular maintenance and inspections to identify potential issues before they become major problems. Owners who have experienced issues with their vehicles are advised to contact Jeep immediately to schedule a repair.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

The recall has raised concerns among owners about the safety and reliability of their vehicles.

While the issue is serious, it is worth noting that the recall is a precautionary measure to prevent potential fires.

Jeep has reported that the issue is rare and that most vehicles will not be affected.

However, owners who have received the recall notice should take it seriously and follow the instructions provided by Jeep.

The company has advised owners to park their vehicles outside and away from structures until the issue is resolved.

Owners can also check the Jeep website for more information on the recall and to schedule a repair.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

The recall is a reminder that even the most reliable vehicles can experience issues. However, it is also a testament to Jeep's commitment to safety and customer satisfaction. While the recall may cause inconvenience to some owners, it is a necessary step to ensure the safety of everyone on the road. In comparison to other vehicle recalls, the Jeep recall is relatively minor, and the company's response has been swift and effective.
