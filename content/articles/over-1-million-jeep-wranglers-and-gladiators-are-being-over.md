## History/Context

The recall affects certain model year 2018-2022 Jeep Wranglers and Gladiators, with the majority of the affected vehicles being in the United States.

The issue is related to the vehicle's fuel pump, which can cause a fire in certain circumstances.

According to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA), the recall was initiated after several reports of fires were received.

The NHTSA has stated that the fires are likely caused by a faulty fuel pump that can overheat and ignite the surrounding fuel.

The recall is expected to begin in the coming weeks, with owners being notified by mail.

## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The technical details of the recall are complex and involve the vehicle's fuel system. The fuel pump is located inside the fuel tank and is responsible for supplying fuel to the engine. In the affected vehicles, the fuel pump can overheat due to a faulty design or manufacturing defect. This can cause the fuel to ignite, leading to a fire. The recall will involve the replacement of the fuel pump and other related components. The table below provides a detailed overview of the affected vehicles and the recall:
