## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Gladiator 4xe was first announced in 2020, with the company touting it as a game-changer in the electric vehicle market.

The Gladiator 4xe was designed to be a hybrid electric vehicle, combining a traditional internal combustion engine with an electric motor.

This design allowed for improved fuel efficiency and reduced emissions, making it an attractive option for environmentally conscious consumers.

However, despite the initial hype, the Gladiator 4xe failed to gain significant traction in the market, leading to Stellantis's decision to cancel the vehicle.

The cancellation of the Gladiator 4xe is a significant blow to the electric vehicle market, as it was seen as a key player in the industry's transition to more sustainable energy sources.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Jeep Gladiator 4xe was equipped with a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine, combined with an electric motor.

This hybrid powertrain produced 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque, making it a formidable performer on and off the road.

The vehicle also featured a advanced suspension system, including a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil suspension with a stabilizer bar in the rear.

This suspension system allowed for improved handling and stability, both on and off the road.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

The Jeep Gladiator 4xe was designed to be a capable off-road vehicle, with its advanced suspension system and hybrid powertrain allowing it to tackle even the most challenging terrain.

In real-world testing, the Gladiator 4xe proved to be a formidable performer, with its electric motor providing instant torque and improved traction.

The vehicle's advanced suspension system also allowed for improved handling and stability, both on and off the road.

However, despite its impressive performance capabilities, the Gladiator 4xe was not without its flaws.

The vehicle's battery pack was relatively small, providing only 25 miles of electric-only range.

This limited range made the vehicle less appealing to consumers who were looking for a more traditional electric vehicle experience.
