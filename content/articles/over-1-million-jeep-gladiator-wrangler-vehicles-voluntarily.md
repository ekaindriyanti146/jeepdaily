## History/Context

The recall affects certain model year 2020-2022 Jeep Gladiator and Wrangler vehicles.

The issue lies in the vehicle's transmission, which can cause the vehicle to shift into neutral without warning, leading to a loss of control.

## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The affected vehicles are equipped with a 3.6L V6 engine and an 8-speed automatic transmission. The transmission's software is the root cause of the problem, as it can malfunction and cause the vehicle to shift into neutral. To rectify the issue, Jeep will update the transmission software and replace the transmission if necessary. The update will prevent the vehicle from shifting into neutral without warning, ensuring a safe driving experience. The following table provides a detailed overview of the affected vehicles:

| Vehicle | Model Year | Engine | Transmission |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Jeep Gladiator | 2020-2022 | 3.6L V6 | 8-speed automatic |
| Jeep Wrangler | 2020-2022 | 3.6L V6 | 8-speed automatic |

The suspension system of the Jeep Gladiator and Wrangler is designed to provide a smooth ride and excellent off-road capabilities. However, the recall does not affect the suspension system, and owners can rest assured that their vehicle's suspension will continue to perform as expected.

The recall has significant implications for Jeep enthusiasts who use their vehicles for off-road adventures.
