## Hook

The automotive world is abuzz with the recent announcement from Jeep that it will be discontinuing the Wrangler and Grand Cherokee 4xe models for the 2026 model year. This move has sent shockwaves through the industry, leaving many to wonder what this means for the future of Jeep's electric and hybrid offerings. As a senior automotive journalist, I will delve into the history and context behind this decision, the technical details of the affected models, their real-world performance, and what this means for the brand and its competitors.

## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler and Grand Cherokee are two of the most iconic models in the Jeep lineup, with a rich history dating back decades.

The Wrangler, in particular, has been a staple of off-road enthusiasts, with its rugged design and capabilities making it a favorite among those who venture into the wilderness.

The Grand Cherokee, on the other hand, has been a more refined offering, catering to those who want a vehicle that can handle both on-road and off-road duties with ease.

The introduction of the 4xe variant brought a new level of efficiency and capability to these models, with its hybrid powertrain providing improved fuel economy and reduced emissions.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Wrangler and Grand Cherokee 4xe models are equipped with a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine, paired with an electric motor and a battery pack.

This powertrain produces a combined 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque, making it a formidable opponent on both paved and unpaved roads.

The suspension system on these models is designed to provide a balance of on-road comfort and off-road capability, with a five-link suspension setup in the front and a multi-link setup in the rear.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

In real-world testing, the Wrangler and Grand Cherokee 4xe have proven to be capable and efficient vehicles. The hybrid powertrain provides smooth and seamless transitions between electric and gasoline power, making it well-suited for both city driving and off-road adventures. The suspension system, while not perfect, provides a comfortable ride on paved roads and can handle moderate off-road terrain with ease. However, the 4xe's weight and complexity have been cited as drawbacks by some owners, who have reported decreased ground clearance and increased maintenance costs.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

The discontinuation of the Wrangler and Grand Cherokee 4xe raises questions about Jeep's commitment to electric and hybrid technology.

While the brand has stated that it will continue to develop and produce alternative fuel vehicles, the 4xe's demise is a significant setback.

In comparison to its competitors, such as the Toyota RAV4 Hybrid and the Ford Escape Hybrid, the 4xe was a unique offering that provided a blend of off-road capability and efficiency.

However, its high price point and limited range may have contributed to its lack of success.

As the automotive industry continues to evolve, it will be interesting to see how Jeep adapts and responds to changing consumer demands and technological advancements.
