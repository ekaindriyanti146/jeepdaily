## The Birth of a Legend
The Jeep's name has been a subject of fascination for many, with its origins tracing back to the early days of World War II. The term 'Jeep' is believed to have originated from the military term 'GP,' which stands for 'General Purpose.' Over time, the pronunciation evolved into 'Jeep,' and the name stuck.

### Powertrain Analysis
The current lineup of Jeep vehicles boasts an impressive array of powertrains, each designed to cater to different driving needs and preferences. ### Axle and Gear Ratios
The axle and gear ratios play a crucial role in determining a vehicle's off-road capability. The Jeep Wrangler, for example, features a Dana 35 rear axle and a Dana 30 front axle, with a gear ratio of 3.45:1. The Gladiator, on the other hand, boasts a Dana 44 rear axle and a Dana 30 front axle, with a gear ratio of 3.73:1.

#### Axle and Gear Ratio Specs
| Vehicle | Rear Axle | Front Axle | Gear Ratio |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Towing Capacity
The towing capacity of a vehicle is a critical factor to consider, especially for those who frequently tow trailers or boats. The Jeep Wrangler, for instance, has a maximum towing capacity of 3,500 pounds, while the Gladiator boasts a maximum towing capacity of 7,650 pounds.
