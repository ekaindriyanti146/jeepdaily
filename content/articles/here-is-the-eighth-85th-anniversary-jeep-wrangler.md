## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Wrangler's history dates back to 1941, when the first Jeep was produced for military use during World War II.

Since then, the Wrangler has evolved significantly, with each generation building upon the legacy of its predecessors.

The Wrangler's design has been influenced by its military roots, with a focus on functionality, simplicity, and off-road capability.

Over the years, the Wrangler has become synonymous with adventure, with its removable tops and doors, fold-down windshield, and rugged construction making it the perfect vehicle for exploring the great outdoors.

The 85th-anniversary edition is a nod to this rich history, with design elements that pay homage to the original Jeep while also incorporating modern features and technologies.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The eighth 85th-anniversary Jeep Wrangler boasts a range of technical features that enhance its off-road capability and on-road comfort.

Under the hood, the Wrangler is powered by a 3.6-liter V6 engine, producing 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque.

This engine is paired with a six-speed manual transmission or an eight-speed automatic, allowing drivers to choose their preferred driving style.

The Wrangler's suspension system features a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link leaf spring suspension in the rear, providing a smooth ride and excellent articulation.

The vehicle also features a range of advanced technologies, including a Uconnect infotainment system, Bluetooth connectivity, and a range of safety features such as electronic stability control and anti-lock braking.
