## History/Context

The Jeep Gladiator 4xe Hybrid Pickup was first introduced in 2020 as a variant of the Gladiator lineup.

It was designed to provide a more environmentally friendly option for customers who still required the capabilities of a pickup truck.

The 4xe moniker denoted the vehicle's hybrid electric powertrain, which combined a turbocharged 2.0-liter inline-four engine with an electric motor.

This setup provided an estimated 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque.

## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Jeep Gladiator 4xe Hybrid Pickup featured a unique powertrain that combined a turbocharged 2.0-liter inline-four engine with an electric motor.

The engine produced 270 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, while the electric motor added an additional 105 horsepower and 130 lb-ft of torque.

The vehicle's eTorque technology allowed for seamless transitions between electric and gasoline power.

The Gladiator 4xe also featured a robust suspension system, including a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil suspension with a stabilizer bar in the rear.

The vehicle's approach and departure angles were 43.6 degrees and 26 degrees, respectively, making it well-suited for off-road adventures.

## Real World Performance

In real-world testing, the Jeep Gladiator 4xe Hybrid Pickup demonstrated impressive capabilities both on and off the road. The Gladiator 4xe also showed impressive towing capabilities, with a maximum towing capacity of up to 6,000 pounds. Off-road, the vehicle's four-wheel drive system and robust suspension allowed it to tackle challenging terrain with ease.
