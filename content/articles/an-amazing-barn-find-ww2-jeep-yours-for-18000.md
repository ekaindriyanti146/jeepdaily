The WW2 Jeep, officially known as the Willys MB, has a rich and storied history. First introduced in 1941, the Jeep was designed to meet the US Army's requirement for a lightweight, versatile, and rugged vehicle that could navigate challenging terrain. Over 360,000 units were produced during the war, with the Jeep playing a crucial role in various military operations. The Jeep's impact extended beyond the battlefield, as it also became an iconic symbol of American ingenuity and determination.
