## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

At the heart of the recall is a concern related to the vehicle's engine and suspension systems.

According to Jeep, certain Gladiator and Wrangler models may be equipped with a faulty engine software calibration, which can cause the vehicle to stall or hesitate under certain conditions.

Additionally, some models may have a suspension component that does not meet the manufacturer's specifications, potentially leading to reduced stability and control.

To address these issues, Jeep will be updating the engine software and replacing the affected suspension components on all recalled vehicles.

| Jeep Gladiator | 2020-2022 | 3.0L EcoDiesel | Front and rear suspension | 5,000 |

## Real World Performance

So, what do these recalls mean for owners and enthusiasts of the Jeep Gladiator and Wrangler? In terms of real-world performance, the impact is likely to be minimal.

Most owners will not notice any significant differences in their vehicle's behavior, and the recall is primarily a precautionary measure to ensure the safety and reliability of these models.

However, it is essential for owners to have their vehicles inspected and updated as soon as possible to avoid any potential issues.

For those who enjoy pushing their vehicles to the limit off-road, the recall may have a slightly more significant impact, as the faulty engine software calibration and suspension component can affect the vehicle's stability and control in extreme conditions.
