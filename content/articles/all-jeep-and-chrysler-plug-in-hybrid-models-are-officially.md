## Section 1: History/Context

The concept of plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs) has been around for decades, but it wasn't until the early 2010s that major automakers began to take notice.

Jeep and Chrysler, under the umbrella of Stellantis, were among the first to dip their toes into the PHEV market.

The Jeep Wrangler 4xe, launched in 2020, was one of the first PHEV models to hit the market.

It was soon followed by the Chrysler Pacifica Hybrid, which offered a unique blend of electric and gasoline power.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Jeep Wrangler 4xe and Chrysler Pacifica Hybrid were both equipped with advanced powertrains that combined electric motors with traditional gasoline engines. The Wrangler 4xe featured a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four cylinder engine, paired with an electric motor and a 17-kWh lithium-ion battery pack. This setup allowed for an estimated 25 miles of electric-only range and a total range of over 400 miles. The Pacifica Hybrid, on the other hand, used a 3.6L V6 engine and a 16-kWh battery pack, providing an estimated 32 miles of electric range.

In terms of suspension, the Wrangler 4xe retained its traditional body-on-frame design, with a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil suspension with a stabilizer bar in the rear. The Pacifica Hybrid, being a minivan, used a more conventional unibody design with a MacPherson strut suspension in the front and a twist-beam axle suspension in the rear.

The technical details of these models were impressive, but they were not without their challenges. The added weight of the battery packs affected the overall handling and performance of the vehicles, and the limited electric range made them less appealing to some consumers.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

In real-world testing, the Jeep Wrangler 4xe and Chrysler Pacifica Hybrid performed admirably. The Wrangler 4xe was able to tackle challenging off-road terrain with ease, thanks to its advanced four-wheel-drive system and electric motor. The Pacifica Hybrid, on the other hand, provided a smooth and quiet ride, making it an excellent choice for families and road trips.

However, the limited electric range of these models was a major concern. In practice, the Wrangler 4xe was only able to achieve around 20 miles of electric range, while the Pacifica Hybrid managed around 25 miles. This was due in part to the weight of the vehicles and the efficiency of the powertrains.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

The demise of Jeep and Chrysler's plug-in hybrid models is a significant blow to the electric vehicle market. While these models had their flaws, they represented an important step towards a more sustainable future. The decision to discontinue these models raises questions about the commitment of major automakers to electrification.

In comparison to other PHEV models on the market, the Jeep Wrangler 4xe and Chrysler Pacifica Hybrid were unique in their approach to electrification. They offered a blend of electric and gasoline power that appealed to a specific segment of consumers. However, their limited electric range and higher price points made them less competitive in the market.

The verdict is still out on the future of electric vehicles at Jeep and Chrysler. While the company has announced plans to invest in new electric models, the discontinuation of their PHEV lineup is a significant setback. As the automotive industry continues to evolve, it will be interesting to see how Jeep and Chrysler adapt to changing consumer demands and regulatory requirements.
