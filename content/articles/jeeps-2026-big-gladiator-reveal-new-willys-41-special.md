The original Willys MB was known for its durability, versatility, and off-road capabilities, qualities that have been carried forward in the modern Jeep lineup.

The Gladiator, introduced in 2019, marked Jeep's return to the pickup truck segment, offering a unique blend of on-road comfort and off-road prowess.

The Willys '41 Special Edition builds upon this foundation, incorporating design elements and capabilities that make it a standout in the market.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

Technically, the 2026 Jeep Gladiator Willys '41 Special Edition is equipped with a 3.6L V6 engine, producing 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque.

It also features an eight-speed automatic transmission and a two-speed transfer case, allowing for seamless transitions between on-road driving and off-road adventures.

The vehicle boasts a ground clearance of 11.1 inches, water fording capability of up to 30 inches, and approach and departure angles of 43.6 and 26 degrees, respectively.

The suspension system includes a five-link coil setup in the front and a five-link coil setup with a stabilizer bar in the rear, ensuring both stability and comfort.
