The Jeep Gladiator was first introduced in 2019 as a 2020 model. It was designed to be a pickup truck version of the iconic Jeep Wrangler, with a focus on off-road capability and ruggedness. The Gladiator was an instant hit, with its unique design, impressive capabilities, and wide range of trim levels and options. Over the years, the Gladiator has undergone several updates and improvements, including the introduction of new engines, transmissions, and technology features. However, with the 2025 model year, Jeep has decided to simplify the Gladiator lineup and offer only one variant.

## Technical Details

The 2025 Jeep Gladiator comes with a 3.6-liter V6 engine, producing 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission and a four-wheel-drive system. The Gladiator also features a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link leaf spring suspension in the rear. The truck has a maximum towing capacity of up to 7,650 pounds and a maximum payload capacity of up to 1,700 pounds.

## Real-World Performance

The 2025 Jeep Gladiator is a capable and rugged truck that is designed to handle a wide range of tasks and terrain. The truck's V6 engine provides plenty of power and torque, making it suitable for towing and hauling heavy loads. The Gladiator's four-wheel-drive system and suspension also make it well-suited for off-road driving, with a high ground clearance and a range of terrain modes to choose from.

In real-world testing, the Gladiator has proven to be a reliable and capable truck, with a smooth ride and responsive handling. The truck's interior is also well-appointed, with a range of premium materials and features.

## Comparison and Verdict

The 2025 Jeep Gladiator is a unique and capable truck that is well-suited for off-road enthusiasts and those who need a rugged and reliable vehicle. While the decision to reduce the Gladiator lineup to just one variant may be disappointing to some, it also simplifies the buying process and makes it easier for buyers to choose the right truck for their needs.

Compared to other trucks in the market, the Gladiator is a standout for its off-road capability and ruggedness. However, it may not be the best choice for those who prioritize on-road comfort and refinement.
