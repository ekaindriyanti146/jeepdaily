## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Jeep Gladiator 4xe was powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four cylinder engine, paired with an electric motor and a 17-kilowatt-hour battery pack. This powertrain produced a total of 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque, making it one of the most powerful vehicles in its class. The 4xe also featured a unique regenerative braking system, which helped to recharge the battery pack and improve overall efficiency. The suspension system was also specially tuned to handle the added weight of the battery pack, while still maintaining the Gladiator's legendary off-road capability.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

In real-world testing, the Jeep Gladiator 4xe proved to be a capable and efficient vehicle. The electric motor provided instant torque and smooth acceleration, making it well-suited for off-road driving. The regenerative braking system also helped to improve overall efficiency, allowing the 4xe to achieve an estimated 50 mpg-e in combined driving. However, the added weight of the battery pack did affect the 4xe's overall handling and performance, making it feel slightly less agile than its gasoline-powered counterpart.

## Section 4: Comparison/Verdict

So, what led to the demise of the Jeep Gladiator 4xe? In comparison to other plug-in hybrid pickup trucks on the market, the 4xe was priced relatively high, with a starting MSRP of over $50,000. Additionally, the 4xe's limited electric-only range and lack of standard features, such as adaptive cruise control, made it less competitive in the market. Ultimately, the Gladiator 4xe was a unique and innovative vehicle, but it failed to gain traction in a crowded and competitive market.
