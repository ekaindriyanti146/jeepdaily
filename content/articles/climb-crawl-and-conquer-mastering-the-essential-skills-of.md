## Powertrain Analysis
The Jeep Wrangler is equipped with a 3.6L V6 engine, producing 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a 6-speed manual transmission or an 8-speed automatic transmission. The Gladiator Truck, on the other hand, features a 3.6L V6 engine with 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, as well as a 6-speed manual transmission or an 8-speed automatic transmission.

### Engine Specifications
The 3.6L V6 engine in the Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator Truck features a cast-iron block, aluminum cylinder heads, and a dual-overhead camshaft design. The engine also features variable valve timing and a multi-point fuel injection system.

#### Engine Upgrades
For those looking to upgrade their engine, there are several options available. A cold air intake system can increase horsepower and torque, while a performance exhaust system can improve engine sound and performance. Additionally, a engine tune can optimize engine performance and increase power output.

## Aftermarket Potential
The Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator Truck have a vast aftermarket potential, with a wide range of parts and accessories available. From lift kits and suspension upgrades to engine modifications and performance exhaust systems, there are countless ways to customize and upgrade these vehicles.

### Lift Kits
A lift kit can increase ground clearance, allowing for larger tires and improved off-road capability. There are several types of lift kits available, including coil spring lift kits, leaf spring lift kits, and spacer lift kits.

#### Suspension Upgrades
Suspension upgrades can improve ride quality and handling, both on and off the road. Upgrades can include coilovers, shocks, and control arms, and can be tailored to specific driving styles and preferences.

## Axle and Gear Ratios
The Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator Truck feature a range of axle and gear ratios, depending on the trim level and transmission. The Wrangler features a 3.21:1 axle ratio in the Sport and Sport S trims, while the Rubicon trim features a 4.10:1 axle ratio. The Gladiator Truck features a 3.73:1 axle ratio in the Sport and Overland trims, while the Rubicon trim features a 4.10:1 axle ratio.

### Axle Upgrades
For those looking to upgrade their axles, there are several options available. A gear ratio change can improve low-end torque and towing capacity, while an axle swap can increase strength and durability.

#### Gear Ratio Change
A gear ratio change can be done by installing a new ring and pinion gear set. This can be a complex and time-consuming process, but can significantly improve vehicle performance.

## Towing Capacity
The Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator Truck have a maximum towing capacity of up to 7,650 pounds, depending on the trim level and transmission. The Wrangler features a maximum towing capacity of up to 3,500 pounds in the Sport and Sport S trims, while the Rubicon trim features a maximum towing capacity of up to 3,000 pounds. The Gladiator Truck features a maximum towing capacity of up to 7,650 pounds in the Overland trim.

### Towing Upgrades
For those looking to upgrade their towing capacity, there are several options available. A towing package can include a hitch, wiring harness, and trailer brake controller, and can increase towing capacity and safety.

#### Trailer Brake Controller
A trailer brake controller can improve towing safety and stability, by allowing the driver to control the trailer brakes. This can be especially useful when towing heavy loads or in hilly terrain.
