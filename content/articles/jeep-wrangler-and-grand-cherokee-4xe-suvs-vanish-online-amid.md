## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The Jeep Wrangler and Grand Cherokee 4xe SUVs are equipped with a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four cylinder engine, paired with an electric motor and a 17-kilowatt-hour battery pack.

This powertrain combination produces an impressive 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque in the Wrangler 4xe, while the Grand Cherokee 4xe delivers 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque as well.

The suspension system in both models is designed to provide a smooth ride on paved roads while maintaining the capability to tackle challenging off-road terrain.

The Wrangler 4xe features a heavy-duty Dana 44 front axle and a Dana 35 rear axle, while the Grand Cherokee 4xe boasts a more advanced Quadra-Trac II 4x4 system with a two-speed transfer case.

In real-world testing, the Jeep Wrangler and Grand Cherokee 4xe SUVs have demonstrated impressive performance and capability.
