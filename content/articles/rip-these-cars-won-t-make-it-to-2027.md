#### Articulation: The Key to Conquering Rocky Terrain
Articulation is the ability of a vehicle to flex and move over uneven terrain, and it's a critical component of Jeep's off-road capability. By allowing the wheels to move independently, Jeep's suspension systems enable the vehicle to maintain contact with the ground, even on the most challenging terrain. This is particularly important when navigating rocky or uneven surfaces, where a vehicle with limited articulation might become stuck or lose traction.

### Suspension Tech: The Science Behind Jeep's Smooth Ride
Jeep's suspension systems are designed to provide a smooth ride on the highway, while still delivering the necessary articulation and flexibility for off-road adventures. The Wrangler's suspension, for example, features a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil suspension with a track bar in the rear. This setup allows for a full 10.9 inches of articulation, making it possible to navigate even the most challenging terrain. The Gladiator, on the other hand, features a more advanced suspension system, with a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil suspension with a track bar and a stabilizer bar in the rear.

### Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is death wobble, and how can I prevent it?** Death wobble is a phenomenon that occurs when a vehicle's suspension becomes unstable, causing the wheels to oscillate wildly. To prevent death wobble, it's essential to ensure that your Jeep's suspension is properly maintained and that you're not exceeding the vehicle's maximum speed or load capacity.
2. **What are lockers, and how do they work?** Lockers are devices that allow you to lock the axles of your Jeep, providing maximum traction and control. By locking the axles, you can ensure that both wheels on an axle are rotating at the same speed, which can be particularly useful in low-traction situations.
3. **What are lift kits, and how do they affect my Jeep's performance?** Lift kits are modifications that raise the suspension of your Jeep, providing increased ground clearance and articulation. While lift kits can be beneficial for off-road performance, they can also affect your Jeep's on-road handling and stability. It's essential to carefully consider the pros and cons before installing a lift kit on your Jeep.
