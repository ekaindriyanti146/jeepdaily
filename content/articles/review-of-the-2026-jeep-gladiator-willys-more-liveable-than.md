## Section 1: History/Context

The Jeep Gladiator has a long and storied history, dating back to the original Gladiator models of the 1960s.

However, the modern Gladiator, introduced in 2019, marked a significant departure from its predecessors, offering a more refined and capable off-road experience.

The Willys edition, named after the iconic Willys-Overland company that originally produced the Jeep, pays homage to this rich heritage while incorporating the latest in automotive technology and design.

## Section 2: Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

Under the hood, the 2026 Jeep Gladiator Willys comes equipped with a 3.6-liter V6 engine, producing 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque.

This powerplant is paired with a six-speed manual transmission or an optional eight-speed automatic, providing drivers with a choice tailored to their preferences.

The suspension is where the Gladiator Willys truly shines, with a five-link coil suspension setup in the front and rear, allowing for impressive articulation and stability both on and off the road.

The Willys edition also includes 32-inch all-terrain tires and a limited-slip rear differential, further enhancing its off-road capabilities.

## Section 3: Real World Performance

In real-world driving, the 2026 Jeep Gladiator Willys proves to be more than just a one-trick pony. On the road, it's surprisingly refined, with a comfortable ride and responsive handling that makes daily driving a pleasure. Off the road, the Gladiator Willys is in its element, capable of tackling challenging terrain with ease thanks to its capable four-wheel-drive system and generous ground clearance. Whether navigating rocky trails, crossing streams, or simply dealing with inclement weather, the Gladiator Willys inspires confidence and delivers on its promise of adventure.
