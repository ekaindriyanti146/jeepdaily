## Hook

The world of off-road enthusiasts was shaken recently with the announcement that Jeep is recalling 112,859 of its Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle (PHEV) SUVs. The reason behind this massive recall is the potential presence of leftover casting sand in the engines of these vehicles. This issue could lead to engine damage, decreased performance, and potentially even cause the engine to fail. As a senior automotive journalist, I believe it's crucial to delve into the history of this issue, the technical details of the affected vehicles, and the real-world implications of this recall.

## History/Context

The recall affects certain models of Jeep's PHEV SUVs, which were produced with a specific engine type.

The leftover casting sand, which is used in the manufacturing process, can sometimes remain in the engine block or cylinder head.

Over time, this sand can cause wear and tear on the engine's components, leading to decreased performance, increased fuel consumption, and potentially even engine failure.

Jeep has stated that it will notify all affected owners and provide repairs free of charge.

The recall is expected to begin soon, with owners being contacted by mail to arrange for the necessary repairs.

## Technical Details/Engine/Suspension

The affected vehicles are equipped with a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine, which is paired with an electric motor to provide a combined output of 375 horsepower. The engine features a cast-aluminum block and cylinder head, which are manufactured using a casting process that involves the use of sand. In some cases, small amounts of sand can remain in the engine block or cylinder head after the casting process is complete. The following table provides more details on the technical specifications of the affected vehicles:

| Vehicle Model | Engine Type | Horsepower | Torque |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Jeep Wrangler 4xe | 2.0L Turbo I4 | 375 hp | 470 lb-ft |
| Jeep Grand Cherokee 4xe | 2.0L Turbo I4 | 375 hp | 470 lb-ft |
| Jeep Gladiator 4xe | 2.0L Turbo I4 | 375 hp | 470 lb-ft |

The suspension system of the affected vehicles features a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil suspension with a stabilizer bar in the rear. The vehicle's off-road capability is enhanced by its high ground clearance, approach and departure angles, and water fording capability.

## Real World Performance

In real-world driving conditions, the presence of leftover casting sand in the engine can cause a range of problems. Some owners have reported decreased engine performance, including a lack of power and rough idling. Others have experienced increased fuel consumption, which can lead to higher operating costs. In extreme cases, the engine can fail, leaving the vehicle stranded and requiring costly repairs. Jeep has stated that it is committed to providing a safe and reliable driving experience for its customers and is taking prompt action to address this issue.

## Comparison/Verdict

Compared to other off-road capable vehicles in its class, the Jeep PHEV SUVs affected by this recall offer impressive performance and capability.

However, the potential presence of leftover casting sand in the engine is a concern that must be addressed.

Jeep's decision to recall the affected vehicles and provide repairs free of charge is a positive step, but it remains to be seen how this issue will affect the long-term reliability and performance of these vehicles.

In conclusion, while the Jeep PHEV SUVs are capable off-road performers, the recall due to potential engine damage from leftover casting sand is a significant issue that must be taken seriously.
