## Overview of the Jeep Gladiator 4xe
The Jeep Gladiator 4xe was a plug-in hybrid variant of the popular Gladiator pickup truck. It was designed to provide a more fuel-efficient and environmentally friendly option for customers who still wanted the capability and off-road prowess of a Jeep. However, due to low demand, the Gladiator 4xe has been canceled.

## Trail Rated Badge and 4x4 Systems
The Gladiator 4xe was still a Trail Rated vehicle, meaning it had to meet certain standards for off-road capability. It featured a four-wheel-drive system with a two-speed transfer case and a rear axle disconnect. The 4xe also had a variety of drive modes, including Hybrid, Electric, and eSave, which allowed the driver to choose how the vehicle used its power.

### Articulation and Suspension Tech
The Gladiator 4xe had a suspension system that was designed to provide a smooth ride on the road while still being capable off-road. It featured a five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil suspension with a rear axle disconnect in the back. The 4xe also had a disconnecting front sway bar, which allowed for greater articulation and flexibility when driving off-road.

## Real-world Trail Test
In real-world testing, the Gladiator 4xe proved to be a capable off-road vehicle. It was able to navigate rocky trails and steep inclines with ease, and its electric motor provided instant torque and quiet operation. However, the 4xe was not without its limitations. Its battery pack was located under the rear seat, which reduced cargo space and made the vehicle less practical for hauling heavy loads.

### Comparison to Other Jeep Models
The Gladiator 4xe was not the only plug-in hybrid Jeep model available. The Wrangler 4xe is still in production and offers many of the same features and capabilities as the Gladiator 4xe. The Wrangler 4xe has a similar powertrain and four-wheel-drive system, but it is a more compact vehicle with a shorter wheelbase and a more agile design.
