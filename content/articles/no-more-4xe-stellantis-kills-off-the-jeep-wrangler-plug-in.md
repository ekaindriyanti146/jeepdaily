## Introduction to the Jeep Wrangler 4xe

The Jeep Wrangler 4xe was a groundbreaking vehicle that combined the classic Wrangler design with a plug-in hybrid powertrain. First introduced in 2020, the Wrangler 4xe was designed to provide a more environmentally friendly alternative to the traditional Wrangler, while still delivering the same level of off-road capability and performance. With its electric motor and gasoline engine, the Wrangler 4xe was capable of achieving an estimated 50 miles per gallon equivalent (MPGe) and could travel up to 25 miles on electric power alone.

## History and Context of the Jeep Wrangler 4xe

The Jeep Wrangler 4xe was a product of Stellantis' efforts to electrify its lineup and reduce its carbon footprint.

The company had announced plans to offer a range of electrified models across its various brands, including Jeep, Chrysler, and Dodge.

The Wrangler 4xe was one of the first models to be introduced as part of this initiative, and it was seen as a key component of Jeep's strategy to appeal to a new generation of environmentally conscious buyers.

Despite its promising start, however, the Wrangler 4xe has struggled to gain traction in the market, and Stellantis has now decided to discontinue the model for the 2026 model year.

## Technical Details of the Jeep Wrangler 4xe

The vehicle featured a number of advanced technologies, including regenerative braking and an e-flite dual-motor automatic transmission.
