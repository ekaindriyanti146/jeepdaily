This iconic vehicle has been revamped to deliver fully fledged off-road capability, posing a formidable challenge to established rivals like the Toyota LandCruiser and Nissan Patrol. The question on everyone's mind is: can the new Pajero live up to its promise and claim a substantial share of the off-road market?

## History and Context

The Mitsubishi Pajero has a rich history dating back to 1982, with its name derived from the Latin word for 'bird of prey'.

Over the years, the Pajero has evolved significantly, with each generation offering improved performance, comfort, and off-road prowess.

The latest iteration is no exception, with Mitsubishi pouring considerable resources into enhancing its off-road credentials.

## Technical Details, Engine, and Suspension

At the heart of the 2026 Mitsubishi Pajero lies a powerful engine, paired with a sophisticated four-wheel-drive system.

The vehicle boasts a robust suspension setup, designed to absorb the shocks and bumps associated with off-road driving.

Some of the key technical details include:
| Feature | Specification |
| --- | --- |
| Engine | 3.0L V6 diesel |
| Power | 220 kW |
| Torque | 550 Nm |
| Transmission | 8-speed automatic |
| Four-wheel-drive system | Super Select 4WD II |
| Suspension | Double wishbone front, multilink rear |
| Ground clearance | 225 mm |
| Approach/departure angles | 36.6°/24.2° |
| Wading depth | 700 mm |

The combination of these technical specifications ensures that the 2026 Mitsubishi Pajero is more than capable of handling the demands of off-road driving, while also providing a comfortable and refined on-road experience.

## Real World Performance

So, how does the 2026 Mitsubishi Pajero perform in real-world off-road scenarios? According to preliminary reports, the vehicle is more than adept at navigating challenging terrain, including rocky inclines, sandy dunes, and muddy tracks. The Super Select 4WD II system proves to be particularly effective, allowing drivers to switch between different modes to suit the conditions. The Pajero's impressive ground clearance, approach and departure angles, and wading depth also make it an ideal choice for those who frequently venture into water crossings or steep inclines.

## Comparison and Verdict

In comparison to its main rivals, the Toyota LandCruiser and Nissan Patrol, the 2026 Mitsubishi Pajero holds its own in terms of off-road capability. While the LandCruiser and Patrol have their strengths, the Pajero's advanced four-wheel-drive system, robust construction, and innovative features make it a compelling alternative. As the off-road market continues to evolve, the new Pajero is well-positioned to capture a significant share of the market.
